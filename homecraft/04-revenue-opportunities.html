Revenue Opportunities

Homecraft Roofing & Property Maintenance
So What?

Your current revenue model is entirely reactive. You wait for the phone to ring, you do the job, you move on. There's nothing wrong with being busy — but reactive businesses are vulnerable to quiet patches, seasonal dips, and competition from tradespeople who market themselves better.

The opportunities below could add significant recurring revenue without requiring you to work harder — just smarter. Most cost nothing to implement. Several can start generating income this week.

Key Insights
⚠

100% Reactive

All your work comes from word of mouth or Bark. No proactive marketing, no recurring contracts, no systematic follow-up.

☆

Zero Reviews Online

No Google or Facebook reviews. Every happy customer who doesn't leave a review is a missed marketing opportunity that costs nothing.

↻

No Repeat Revenue

No maintenance contracts, no seasonal service packages, no systematic way to bring past customers back for additional work.

▲

Upsell Potential

You offer a wide range of services but customers typically only know about the one they called about. Every job is a chance to identify additional work.

11 Opportunities

These are ordered by impact and ease of implementation. The first five can all be started this week at zero cost. Click each one to see the details.

1

Review Push Campaign

High Revenue Impact Free Start This Week
▶

Reviews are the single most powerful free marketing tool for a trade business. A roofer with 30+ five-star Google reviews will win work over one with zero reviews, every time — even if they charge more.

  • Send a WhatsApp message to every customer after every job asking for a Google review
  • We've written the exact templates in the Ready-Made Assets tab
  • Target: 5 reviews in the first fortnight, 20+ within 3 months
  • Reply to every single review — it shows you're active and professional
  • Once you have 10+ reviews, they become self-sustaining — new customers see them and leave their own
2

Seasonal Campaigns

High Revenue Impact Free Start This Week
▶

Roofing and property maintenance is seasonal, and you should market accordingly. Don't wait for customers to realise they need gutter cleaning in autumn — tell them in September.

  • Spring: Roof inspections after winter damage, exterior painting, high pressure cleaning
  • Summer: New roofs and major works (best weather), exterior painting, cladding
  • Autumn: Gutter cleaning, roof checks before winter, pointing and repairs
  • Winter: Emergency repairs, interior painting, chimney work
  • Post a seasonal reminder on Facebook every month — we've provided the calendar in Ready-Made Assets
3

Referral Programme

High Revenue Impact Low Cost
▶

Your best customers already recommend you. A referral programme formalises this and gives them a reason to do it more often.

  • Offer a reward for every referral that converts into a paid job — e.g. £25 off their next job, a free gutter clean, or a case of beer
  • Print referral cards to leave with every customer (see Ready-Made Assets)
  • Mention it at the end of every job: "If you know anyone else who needs work doing, we'd appreciate the recommendation — and we'll sort you out with a thank-you"
  • Track referrals in a simple spreadsheet so you know who to thank
4

Upsell During Jobs

High Revenue Impact Free Start This Week
▶

You're already on the roof. You can see things the customer can't. This is the perfect moment to identify additional work and offer it on the spot.

  • Before starting any job, do a quick visual check of the whole roof, gutters, pointing, and chimney
  • Take photos of anything that needs attention and show the customer
  • Frame it as helpful, not salesy: "While I was up there I noticed your pointing is starting to go on the left side. Not urgent, but worth keeping an eye on. I can give you a price if you want."
  • We've written a conversation script in the Ready-Made Assets tab
  • Even a 20% upsell rate would significantly increase your average job value
5

Service Bundling

Medium–High Impact Free
▶

Package multiple services together at a slight discount. Customers love perceived value, and it increases your average job size while reducing your per-job overhead (one trip, one setup).

  • Winter Ready Package: Gutter cleaning + roof inspection + pointing check
  • Spring Refresh: High pressure cleaning + gutter clean + exterior touch-ups
  • Property Health Check: Full roof inspection + gutter check + pointing assessment + written report
  • Price the bundle 10–15% less than individual services — you still make more because it's one visit
  • We've designed the bundle cards in Ready-Made Assets
6

Annual Maintenance Contracts

Recurring Revenue Medium Effort
▶

Offer annual maintenance contracts to homeowners: one visit in spring, one in autumn, covering gutter cleaning, roof inspection, and minor repairs. This creates predictable, recurring revenue.

  • Price at £150–250/year depending on property size
  • Two visits: Spring (post-winter check) and Autumn (pre-winter prep)
  • Include gutter cleaning, roof inspection, minor pointing repairs, and a written condition report
  • Customers on contracts get priority booking for any additional work
  • Target: 20 contracts in Year 1 = £3,000–5,000 guaranteed annual revenue
7

Emergency Callout Premium

Higher Margins Free
▶

If you respond to emergency calls (storm damage, leaks), you can charge a premium for out-of-hours or same-day service. Customers expect to pay more for urgent work.

  • Publish an emergency callout number (can be the same number)
  • Set a minimum callout fee for emergencies (e.g. £75–100 plus work)
  • Advertise this on your Facebook page and Google Business Profile
  • After storms, post on Facebook: "Storm damage? We're available for emergency roof repairs. Call 07455 544085"
8

Landlord & Letting Agent Partnerships

Recurring Revenue Medium Effort
▶

Landlords and letting agents need reliable tradespeople for ongoing maintenance across multiple properties. One good relationship can generate regular work for years.

  • Identify local letting agents and landlord Facebook groups in the North East
  • Offer a "landlord rate" — a small discount in exchange for regular, guaranteed work
  • Offer priority response times for their tenants
  • Drop in to local letting agents with your business card and a brief introduction
  • Target: 3–5 landlord relationships generating 2–3 jobs each per quarter
9

Local Business Partnerships

Lead Generation Free
▶

Partner with complementary local businesses: electricians, plumbers, plasterers, estate agents. You refer customers to them, they refer customers to you.

  • Find 3–5 non-competing tradespeople you trust and set up mutual referral agreements
  • Keep their business cards in your van; ask them to keep yours
  • When a customer asks "do you know a good plumber?", you've got an answer — and that plumber sends roofing work your way
  • Estate agents are particularly valuable — they're constantly seeing properties that need roof work
10

Photography Portfolio

Supports All Other Opportunities Free Start This Week
▶

You already take photos of your work — this is about doing it systematically. Every job should be documented with before, during, and after photos. This content feeds everything else: Facebook posts, Google listings, website, flyers.

  • Before starting: Take 2–3 photos of the problem area
  • During: Quick shot of work in progress (shows effort and process)
  • After: 2–3 shots of the finished result from good angles
  • Store them in a simple phone folder labelled by job type and area
  • Ask permission to use them (use the WhatsApp template from the Social Media Audit)
11

Facebook Marketplace

Lead Generation Free
▶

Facebook Marketplace isn't just for selling furniture. You can list your services there and reach people who might never find your business page.

  • Create a listing for each of your key services (roof repair, gutter cleaning, pointing, etc.)
  • Set the price as "Free Estimate" or a starting-from price
  • Include your best before-and-after photos
  • Renew the listings every few weeks to keep them fresh
  • This works surprisingly well for trades in local areas — people browse Marketplace daily
