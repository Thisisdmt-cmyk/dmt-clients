The Plan

Ten steps to transform your Facebook presence from invisible to professional. Steps 1–3 are critical — do those this week. The rest can follow over the next fortnight. Total time investment: about 2–3 hours spread across a couple of evenings.

1

Create a Facebook Business Page

20 mins Critical
▶

This is the most important step. Go to facebook.com/pages/create and set up a proper business page. Choose "Local Business" as the category and "Roofing Service" as the sub-category.

  • Use the exact name: Homecraft Roofing & Property Maintenance (correct spelling)
  • Set your location to North East England
  • Add your phone number: 07455 544085
  • You can keep your personal profile — the business page is separate
  • Once created, share the page from your personal profile to let your existing contacts know
2

Fix the Business Name Spelling

5 mins Critical
▶

Your personal profile currently says "Maintainance" instead of "Maintenance". When you create the business page, make sure you use the correct spelling. Also update your personal profile name to fix the typo.

  • Correct: Homecraft Roofing & Property Maintenance
  • Wrong: Homecraft Roofing & Property Maintainance
  • This matters for search — people type the correct spelling when looking for services
3

Complete the About Section

10 mins Critical
▶

Your About section is completely empty. We've written the text for you in the Ready-Made Assets tab — just copy and paste it. This tells Facebook what your business does and helps you appear in local searches.

  • Go to your page → About → Edit
  • Paste the pre-written text from our Assets tab
  • Add your phone number, service area, and opening hours
4

Upload a Professional Profile Picture

10 mins High
▶

Your profile picture needs to work at tiny sizes — it appears as a small circle next to every post, comment, and message. Ideally use a simple logo or a clean photo of your van/livery.

  • Minimum size: 170 × 170 pixels
  • Best option: A clean logo with "Homecraft" readable at small sizes
  • Alternative: A photo of your van with livery visible
  • Avoid: Group photos, scenery, or anything with small text
5

Add a Strong Cover Photo

10 mins High
▶

The cover photo is the banner across the top of your page. Use your best completed job photo — ideally a drone shot or a wide-angle of a finished roof.

  • Ideal size: 820 × 312 pixels
  • Include your phone number (07455 544085) as text overlay
  • Add 2–3 key services: "Roofing | Property Maintenance | Free Quotes"
  • Canva (free) has Facebook cover templates you can customise in minutes
6

Migrate Your Best Photos to the Business Page

30 mins High
▶

Your personal profile has good work photos. Re-post the best 10–15 as individual posts on your new business page. Each one should have a brief description of the job.

  • Before & after shots are the most powerful — prioritise those
  • Add location and service type to each post
  • Use the post templates in the Ready-Made Assets tab
  • Space them out: 2–3 per day over a week, not all at once
7

Set Up a Username & Vanity URL

5 mins Medium
▶

Once you have 25 followers (or sometimes immediately), you can claim a custom URL like facebook.com/HomecraftRoofing. This looks far more professional than a random string of numbers.

  • Go to Settings → General → Username
  • Try: @HomecraftRoofing or @HomecraftRoofingNE
  • This becomes your shareable link for everything — business cards, van livery, flyers
8

Invite Your Personal Contacts to Like the Page

10 mins High
▶

Facebook lets you invite all your friends to like your new business page. This gives you an instant audience and social proof.

  • Go to your business page → Invite Friends
  • Select all and send invites
  • Share the page on your personal timeline with a post explaining you've set up a proper business page
  • Ask family and close friends to share it too — the first 48 hours of momentum matter
9

Commit to 2 Posts Per Week

20 mins/week High
▶

Consistency beats perfection. Two posts a week keeps your page alive and visible. A simple routine:

  • Tuesday: Before & after of a recent job, or a roofing tip
  • Friday: Customer shoutout, service spotlight, or behind-the-scenes photo

Take photos during the working day. Spend 5 minutes writing the post at the end of the day. We've written five ready-made posts in the Assets tab to get you started.

10

Ask 5 Happy Customers for Reviews

5 mins each High
▶

Once your business page is live, reviews become your most powerful marketing tool. People trust other people's experiences more than any advert.

  • After every job, send the customer a quick WhatsApp message asking for a review
  • We've written the exact messages in the Ready-Made Assets tab — just copy, paste, and send
  • Aim for 5 new reviews in the first fortnight
  • Always reply to every review — a simple "Thanks, glad we could help!" goes a long way
