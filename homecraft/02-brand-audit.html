Name Consistency

Needs Fixing

Your business name appears differently across platforms. On Facebook it's "Homecraft Roofing & Property Maintainance" (misspelled). On Bark it may be slightly different again. Inconsistency confuses customers and dilutes your brand. You need one official name used everywhere, every time.

~

Logo

Needs Improvement

You don't appear to have a formal, professional logo that's used consistently. A good logo doesn't need to be expensive — but it does need to be clean, readable at small sizes (think Facebook profile picture, business card, van door), and used everywhere without variation.

~

Colour Palette

Good Instinct, Not Formalised

Orange is a strong choice for a trade business — it stands out, feels energetic, and differentiates you from the sea of blue and red trade logos. The problem is it's not formalised: no specific hex code, no consistent usage, no defined supporting colours. Locking this down is easy and costs nothing.

~

Printed Materials / Flyer

Functional but Inconsistent

Any existing flyers or printed materials may not align with a consistent brand identity. Without a locked-in logo, colour palette, and name format, every new print run risks looking different from the last one. This makes you look less established than you are.

Online Presence

Needs Fixing

No website, a personal Facebook profile instead of a business page, and the name misspelled online. Your online brand presence is essentially non-existent as a cohesive identity. When a potential customer Googles "Homecraft Roofing" they should find a consistent, professional presence — right now they won't.

4

Get a Professional Logo Designed

£50 – £150 High
▶

A clean, professional logo used consistently across everything is the single biggest visual upgrade you can make. It doesn't need to be complicated — in fact, simpler is better for trades.

  • Use your orange (#E87722) as the primary colour
  • Must be readable at very small sizes (social media profile picture)
  • Get it in vector format (SVG/AI) so it can be scaled to any size
  • Options: Fiverr (£20–50), local designer (£80–150), or Canva DIY (free)
  • Request versions: full colour, white on dark, dark on light, icon-only
5

Vehicle Livery

£200 – £500 High
▶

Your van is a mobile billboard. Branded livery is one of the best investments a tradesperson can make — it builds trust when you pull up to a job and generates enquiries while you're parked or driving.

  • Include: logo, business name, phone number, key services, "Free Quotes"
  • Use your brand colours (orange, white, dark grey)
  • Keep it clean and readable from a distance
  • Magnetic signs are a cheaper option (£40–80) if you're not ready for full wraps
6

Branded Workwear

£50 – £150 Medium
▶

Branded polo shirts, hoodies, or softshell jackets with your logo and phone number. Makes you look professional, builds trust with customers, and serves as free advertising when you're on-site or popping into the merchant.

  • Start with 3–5 polo shirts and a branded hoodie
  • Embroidered logos look more professional than printed ones
  • Local embroidery shops are often cheaper than online — around £8–15 per garment for logo embroidery
7

Business Cards

£20 – £40 Medium
▶

Simple, professional business cards to leave with every customer, drop through letterboxes, and hand out at merchants and local businesses.

  • Front: Logo, name, phone number, key services
  • Back: Full service list or "Free Quotes — Call 07455 544085"
  • Use your brand colours
  • 500 cards from Vistaprint or similar: £20–40
8

Professional Flyer / Leaflet Refresh

£30 – £80 Medium
▶

Once your logo and colours are locked in, create a professional A5 flyer that you can distribute through letterboxes, leave at local shops, and hand to every customer.

  • Include: logo, full service list, phone number, areas covered, "Free Quotes"
  • Use photos of your best work (before & after shots)
  • Design on Canva (free) and print via Vistaprint or a local printer
  • 250 flyers: around £30–50 for professional quality glossy print
