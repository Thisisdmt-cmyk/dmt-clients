Ten prioritised steps to transform your social media presence. Start at the top — the critical and high-priority actions will have the biggest impact on getting new customers through the door. Click any step to see exactly what to do.

1
Create a Pinterest Business Account with 10 Boards
Critical

Pinterest is the number one platform for home improvement inspiration. Homeowners and interior designers search Pinterest when planning a room — they're already in buying mode when they find you there.

What to do:

  • Go to business.pinterest.com and sign up using your business email
  • Set your business name to "Whitburn Coastal Flooring" and category to "Home Decor"
  • Add your showroom address, phone number, and a link to your Facebook page
  • Create these 10 boards: Karndean Inspiration, Statement Staircases, Open Plan Living, Bedroom Comfort, Designer Carpets, Before & After, Colour Palettes, Whitburn Showroom, Flooring Tips, Our Work
  • Upload your best 5 work photos to each relevant board (descriptions are ready in the Assets tab)
Flooring is one of the most visual purchases a homeowner makes. Pinterest puts your best work in front of people who are actively planning and ready to buy. Your competitors aren't doing this yet — move first.
2
Set Up a Content Calendar — 3 Posts Per Week Minimum
High

Irregular posting tells the algorithm (and potential customers) that you're not active. Consistent posting — even just 3 times a week — keeps you visible in feeds and builds trust.

What to do:

  • Pick 3 fixed days per week (e.g., Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday)
  • Use the 30-day content calendar in the Assets tab — it's already planned out for you
  • Batch your photos: take 5–10 photos on a fitting day, then schedule them across the week
  • Mix content types: work photos, designer brand showcases, behind-the-scenes, tips, customer stories
Social media algorithms reward consistency. Three posts per week keeps your business visible without feeling like a full-time job. The content calendar takes the guesswork away.
3
Start Instagram Reels — Flooring Transformation Timelapses
High

Instagram Reels get 2–3 times more reach than standard posts. Flooring transformations are perfect for this format — people love watching a room change from bare boards to a finished floor.

What to do:

  • Before your next fitting, prop your phone up in the corner of the room on timelapse mode
  • Record the full transformation from bare floor to finished room
  • Upload to Instagram Reels with a caption (ready-made captions are in the Assets tab)
  • Add trending audio — Instagram suggests popular music when you create a Reel
  • Aim for 1 Reel per week from your fitting jobs
One timelapse video of a Karndean kitchen installation can reach thousands of local homeowners. It showcases your skill in 30 seconds and makes people imagine their own home looking that good.
4
Launch a Review Generation System
High

You have zero Google reviews despite a perfect score on Facebook. Your happy customers just need a nudge. The templates in the Assets tab make it effortless.

What to do:

  • After every fitting, send the WhatsApp review request message (pre-written in Assets tab)
  • Include a direct link to your Google review page so they can tap and type
  • Follow up once after 3 days if they haven't left one — the follow-up template is also ready
  • Aim for 4 new Google reviews per month — that's just 1 per week
  • When a review comes in, reply to it publicly (response templates are in Deliverable 02)
Google reviews are the number one trust signal for local businesses. Going from 0 to 10+ reviews will dramatically improve where you appear in local search results. Your customers already love you — you just need to ask them.
5
Complete Your Facebook About Section
Medium

Your Facebook About section is the first thing many potential customers read. An incomplete profile looks unprofessional and means people can't find key details like your opening hours or brands you stock.

What to do:

  • Go to your Facebook page → About → Edit each section
  • Copy and paste the complete About section text from the Assets tab
  • Add your opening hours: Tue–Fri 9am–4pm, Wed 9am–1pm, Sat 10am–2pm
  • Set your address to 1 East Street / 4 North Guards, Whitburn Village, SR6
  • Add your phone: 0191 529 5905
  • Set your category to "Carpet & Flooring Store"
A complete profile builds trust and makes it easier for people to contact you. It takes 5 minutes but immediately makes your page look more professional.
6
Optimise Your Instagram Bio and Hashtag Strategy
Medium

Your Instagram bio is your shopfront on the platform — it needs to tell people who you are, what you do, and how to visit in three seconds flat.

What to do:

  • Update your bio using the ready-made text in the Assets tab
  • Use the hashtag sets from the Assets tab on every post — choose the set that matches your content type
  • Add your showroom address to your profile location
  • Include a call-to-action: "Visit our showroom" or "DM for a free quote"
The right hashtags put your posts in front of people who don't follow you yet but are searching for flooring inspiration. The right bio converts them from viewers to visitors.
7
Cross-Post TikTok Content to Instagram Reels
Medium

You're already creating video content for TikTok — make it work twice as hard by posting the same videos to Instagram Reels.

What to do:

  • Save your TikTok videos to your phone before posting (use "Save to device")
  • Upload the same video to Instagram Reels with a tailored caption
  • Remove any TikTok watermark first — Instagram reduces reach on watermarked content
  • Adjust captions and hashtags for each platform (templates in Assets tab)
One piece of content, two audiences. This doubles your reach with almost no extra effort. Many of your potential customers are on Instagram but not TikTok, and vice versa.
8
Pin All Your Best Work Photos to Pinterest Boards
Medium

Once your Pinterest boards are set up (Step 1), populate them with your existing library of work photos. You've got 164 Instagram posts worth of content — repurpose the best ones.

What to do:

  • Go through your Instagram and Facebook photos and save the best 30–40 images
  • Upload each to the most relevant Pinterest board
  • Use the pin descriptions from the Assets tab — they're written to be searchable
  • Add relevant keywords: flooring type, brand name, room type, style
  • Aim for at least 5 pins per board to start
Pinterest pins are evergreen — unlike social media posts, they keep getting found for months and years. A pin you upload today could bring a customer to your door in six months' time.
9
Explore Facebook and Instagram Ads for Showroom Awareness
Low

Once your profiles are polished and you're posting consistently, a small ad budget can amplify your reach significantly — especially for driving showroom visits.

What to do:

  • Start with just £5–10 per week on boosted Facebook posts
  • Target: 10-mile radius around Whitburn Village, homeowners aged 30–65
  • Boost your best-performing posts (the ones that already get the most likes and comments)
  • Use "Visit our showroom" as your call-to-action
  • Track results for one month before increasing spend
Get the fundamentals right first (Steps 1–8), then amplify with paid reach. A small, targeted budget goes a long way for a local business. This is a future step once organic content is consistent.
10
Set Up an Automated Posting Schedule
Low

Once you're comfortable creating regular content, scheduling tools let you batch your posts in one sitting and have them publish automatically throughout the week.

What to do:

  • Facebook has a free built-in scheduler: create your post, click the dropdown next to "Post", and choose "Schedule"
  • Meta Business Suite (free) lets you schedule both Facebook and Instagram from one place
  • Spend 30 minutes on a Monday morning scheduling all your posts for the week
  • For Pinterest, the free plan on Tailwind or Pinterest's own scheduler works well
Scheduling means you're never scrambling for content at the last minute. One focused session per week keeps all your platforms active without daily pressure. This step saves you time once the content habit is established.
