Phase 1 — Free, This Week
1

Claim & Optimise Your Google Business Profile

Free Urgent This Week
▼

This is the single most important thing you can do for your business online. A claimed, optimised Google Business Profile means you appear on Google Maps, in local search results, and in the “local pack” (the map section at the top of Google search). It’s completely free.

Step-by-step (do this on your phone or computer):

  1. Go to business.google.com
  2. Sign in with a Google account (create one if needed — use your business email or a new Gmail)
  3. Search for your business: type “Whitburn Coastal Flooring” — if a listing appears, click “Claim this business”
  4. If no listing appears: click “Add your business to Google” and enter your details
  5. Verify your business: Google will ask you to verify — usually by phone call or postcard to 0191 529 5905. The phone call is instant; the postcard takes 5-7 days.
  6. Once verified: you can edit all your business information, add photos, respond to reviews, and post updates

Time needed: 15 minutes to claim, then 30 minutes to fill in the details (we’ve written everything for you in the Ready-Made Assets tab).

3

Start Generating Google Reviews

Free Urgent This Week
▼

You have 8 Facebook reviews at 100% recommendation — your customers clearly love you. But Google doesn’t know that. New Image Carpets has 46 Google reviews at 4.9 stars. That’s the target.

Your review campaign:

  1. Get your Google Review link: Once your profile is claimed, go to your Google Business dashboard > “Ask for reviews” > copy the short link
  2. Text or WhatsApp your recent customers: Use the ready-made message templates in the Assets tab. Start with the last 10-15 customers you’ve done work for.
  3. Ask at the point of delight: The best time to ask is right after you’ve finished a job and the customer is happy. “If you’re happy with how it looks, we’d really appreciate a Google review — it makes a huge difference to a small business.”
  4. Target: 4 reviews per month. Within 6 months you’ll have 24+ reviews and be competitive with every flooring business in the area.

Important: Never offer incentives for reviews (Google’s terms prohibit this). Just ask honestly — happy customers are usually glad to help.

Get Listed on Houzz

Free
▼

Houzz is critical for premium flooring. This is where interior designers, architects, and homeowners planning renovations go to find professionals. If you stock designer brands like Margo Selby and Karndean, this is your audience.

  1. Go to houzz.co.uk/professionals
  2. Create a free professional profile
  3. Category: “Flooring” or “Carpet & Flooring”
  4. Upload your best showroom and installation photos
  5. Write a description emphasising your designer brand partnerships
  6. Houzz leads tend to be higher value — these are customers planning significant renovations, not looking for the cheapest price
6

Get Listed on Checkatrade

Low Cost
▼

Checkatrade is the UK’s leading trusted trader platform. For fitting services, having a Checkatrade profile adds serious trust — customers feel safer knowing you’ve been vetted.

  • Go to checkatrade.com/join
  • Membership costs approximately £60-120/month depending on your area and categories
  • You’ll need to provide proof of insurance, qualifications, and references
  • The Checkatrade badge is a powerful trust signal for customers who don’t know you yet
  • Reviews on Checkatrade are verified, which makes them more trustworthy than most platforms

Consider this an investment, not an expense. A single job from a Checkatrade lead will pay for several months of membership.

Phase 3 — Future Investment
8

Professional Website

Investment: £1,200–£2,500
▼

A full website is the long-term goal. Once your Google Profile is working and generating reviews, a website becomes the next step to truly competing with businesses like New Image Carpets.

What a good flooring website needs:

  • Homepage with showroom photos and brand logos
  • Individual pages for each flooring type (carpet, LVT, laminate, vinyl, hardwood)
  • A gallery of completed installations (before & after)
  • Brand partner pages (Karndean, Alternative Flooring, Margo Selby, Invictus, iSENSE)
  • Contact page with embedded Google Map, phone number, and an enquiry form
  • “Book a Home Visit” or “Request a Quote” call-to-action on every page
  • Customer testimonials and review excerpts
  • SEO-optimised content for local search terms

Budget guide: £1,200–£1,800 for a solid template-based site (Squarespace, WordPress). £2,000–£2,500 for a bespoke design. Avoid anyone quoting under £800 — you’ll get what you pay for.

This is not included in this package — but everything we’ve done here (brand colours, SEO descriptions, service lists, photo guidance) gives any web designer a massive head start. You’ll save hundreds on briefing alone.

Photo Guidance

Google profiles with photos get 42% more requests for directions and 35% more click-throughs to websites. Upload at least 10 photos to start. Use this checklist.

  • Showroom Interior — Wide Shot
    Stand at the entrance and capture the full showroom. Good lighting, tidy displays. Show the range and scale of what you stock.
  • Showroom Interior — Sample Displays
    Close-up of sample boards, racks, and stands. Show the variety. Customers want to see they'll have plenty to choose from.
  • Karndean Display Area
    If you have a dedicated Karndean section, photograph it. Brand association boosts credibility.
  • Alternative Flooring / Margo Selby Samples
    Showcase the designer collections. These attract premium customers.
  • Fitting in Progress
    A photo of professional fitting work. Shows you're not just a shop — you do the full job. Wear branded clothing if possible.
  • Completed Installation — Kitchen/Living Room
    A beautifully finished room. Clean, well-lit, showing the flooring as the star. Before-and-after pairs are even better.
  • Completed Installation — Staircase
    Staircase carpets are your signature work. Photograph your best staircase installations — especially Margo Selby patterns.
  • Shop Exterior / Whitburn Village
    Your shop front from the street. Include the village setting — it shows you're a real, established local business.
  • Team / Staff Photo
    People trust people. A friendly photo of you (and your team, if applicable) in the showroom makes the business feel approachable.
  • Home Visit / Consultation
    If you can photograph a home visit (with customer permission), this shows the personal service that sets you apart from chain stores.
Photo Tips
  • Lighting matters most. Natural light or good showroom lighting. Avoid flash — it flattens textures.
  • Clean and tidy. Remove clutter before shooting. Every photo represents your business.
  • Landscape orientation works best for Google Business Profile cover photos.
  • Phone cameras are fine — modern phones take excellent photos. Just wipe the lens first.
  • Show texture and detail — flooring is tactile. Get close enough to show the quality.
