How This Works — Financial & Legal

This is a straightforward trade between two professionals. Here's how both parties handle it cleanly.

The Trade Structure

1.

Both parties invoice each other at the agreed trade value — which is the cost of materials at trade price. Each party absorbs their own labour costs as their contribution to the arrangement.

2.

Invoices are set at matching values and contra each other. No cash changes hands.

3.

Both parties declare the trade income on their respective tax returns and claim the contra invoice as a business expense.

4.

Both parties retain invoices and this agreement for 6 years as required by HMRC.

VAT Status Declaration

If either party is VAT-registered, VAT must be charged on their invoice. The agreed trade value should be set inclusive of any VAT to ensure no additional cash is required.

Tax Reporting

DMT: Declares trade income on Self Assessment (SA103). Claims flooring invoice as business expense.

Whitburn Coastal: Declares trade income in business accounts. Claims DMT invoice as marketing/consultancy expense.

Each party is solely responsible for their own tax affairs. Neither party provides tax advice to the other.

Important Notes

  • → This is a barter/trade arrangement as recognised by HMRC. Both supplies are taxable trading income.
  • → The agreed trade value represents materials at trade price. Both parties contribute their professional labour at no additional charge.
  • → We recommend both parties consult their own accountant before signing to confirm the arrangement suits their individual tax position.
  • → This agreement does not constitute financial or tax advice.
