Professional Email Setup Guide

Step-by-step instructions for getting your own professional email domain. This replaces your Outlook address with something like hello@whitburncoastalflooring.co.uk.

Step 1: Register Your Domain

Go to a domain registrar like 123-reg.co.uk, GoDaddy.co.uk, or Namecheap.com. Search for whitburncoastalflooring.co.uk. If available, register it. Cost: around £5–10 per year for a .co.uk domain. This also secures the domain for a future website.

Step 2: Choose an Email Provider

Option A (Simplest): Most domain registrars include basic email hosting. 123-reg and GoDaddy offer email packages from £1–3/month.
Option B (Best): Google Workspace (£5/month) gives you a Gmail interface with your custom domain. If you’re comfortable with Gmail, this is the best option.
Option C (Free): Some registrars include email forwarding for free — emails sent to hello@whitburncoastalflooring.co.uk get forwarded to your Outlook. You can then set Outlook to send replies “from” your new address.

Step 3: Set Up Your Email Address

Create your main address. We recommend: hello@whitburncoastalflooring.co.uk. Alternatives: info@, enquiries@, or your first name. Keep it simple and easy to say over the phone.

Step 4: Set Up Forwarding

Forward your new email to your existing Outlook address. This way you don’t need to check two inboxes — everything lands in the same place. The only thing that changes is the address customers see.

Step 5: Update Everywhere

Update your email address on: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Google Business Profile, business cards, showroom signage, quote templates, and anywhere else it appears. This can be done gradually — both addresses will work during the transition.
