Managing Customers

Every person who walks into your showroom, calls for a quote, or messages on Facebook is a potential customer. The app keeps them all in one place.

Adding a Customer
  1. Tap the Customers tab at the bottom of the app
  2. Tap the "+ Add Customer" button
  3. Fill in the details (see field guide below)
  4. Tap "Save Customer"
Field-by-Field Guide
FieldWhat to enter
NameCustomer's full name. First and last, e.g. "Sarah Johnson"
PhoneTheir mobile number. This powers the one-tap Call and WhatsApp buttons
AddressFull address including postcode. Useful for planning home visits and fitting schedules
EmailOptional but useful for follow-ups. If they don't offer one, leave it blank
Property TypeHouse, flat, bungalow, or commercial. Helps you remember the property when quoting
NotesAnything useful — "Referred by Mrs Thompson", "Has a large dog", "Prefers carpet samples brought to home"
Search, Edit, Call & WhatsApp

Search: Use the search bar at the top of the Customers list. Search by name, phone number, or address.

Edit: Tap any customer to open their details, then tap "Edit" to update any field.

Call: Tap the phone icon next to any customer — your phone dials them instantly.

WhatsApp: Tap the WhatsApp icon to open a chat with that customer. The app uses their phone number to open WhatsApp directly.

Pro Tip

Add every customer — even enquiries that don't convert. They might come back in 6 months when they're ready. Having their details means you can follow up.

Creating Quotes

This is the heart of the app. Build professional, room-by-room quotes with automatic calculations, then share them via WhatsApp in seconds.

Full Quote Walkthrough
  1. Select a customer — go to the Quotes tab and tap "+ New Quote". Choose an existing customer or add a new one
  2. Add a room — tap "+ Add Room" and select the room name (Kitchen, Hallway, Bedroom 1, etc.)
  3. Enter dimensions — type the room length and width in metres. The app calculates the area automatically (with 10% wastage built in)
  4. Select flooring type and brand — choose from Carpet, LVT, Laminate, Vinyl, or Hardwood, then select the brand (Karndean, iSENSE, etc.)
  5. Check price per m² — the app auto-fills from your default pricing in Settings. You can override it for this quote if needed
  6. Add extras — tick any that apply: underlay, door bars, furniture moving, uplift of old flooring, subfloor prep, skirting removal, stair rods
  7. Add more rooms — tap "+ Add Room" again to quote for additional rooms on the same job
  8. Review the total — the app shows materials, fitting, extras, subtotal, VAT, and grand total
  9. Share via WhatsApp — tap "Share Quote" to send a clean, branded summary to the customer via WhatsApp
Worked Example

Mrs Johnson wants Karndean Knight Tile in her kitchen (4m × 5m) and hallway (3m × 1.2m).

Room 1 — Kitchen:

  • Area: 4 × 5 = 20m²
  • With 10% wastage: 22m²
  • Karndean Knight Tile at £35/m² = £770 materials
  • Fitting at £15/m² = £330 fitting
  • Underlay: £80 | Door bars: £25
  • Kitchen total: £1,205

Room 2 — Hallway:

  • Area: 3 × 1.2 = 3.6m²
  • With 10% wastage: 3.96m² (rounded to 4m²)
  • Karndean Knight Tile at £35/m² = £140 materials
  • Fitting at £15/m² = £60 fitting
  • Door bars: £25
  • Hallway total: £225

Quote Total: £1,430 + VAT = £1,716

The app does all of this automatically. You just type in the room sizes.

Quote Status Tracking

Every quote has a status. Tap the status badge on any quote to move it forward:

Draft→ Sent→ Accepted→ Booked→ Complete→ Paid

This lets you see at a glance where every quote stands — how many are waiting for a response, how many are booked in, and what's been paid.

Pro Tip

Always share the quote within an hour of the home visit. Speed wins. The longer you wait, the more likely the customer gets a price from someone else first.

Tracking Jobs

Once a quote is accepted and booked, it becomes a job. The Jobs tab tracks everything from scheduling through to payment.

How Jobs Link to Quotes

When you update a quote status to "Booked", the app automatically creates a job entry. The job inherits all the details from the quote — customer, rooms, flooring types, and pricing. You don't need to re-enter anything.

Job Status Flow

Track every job through these stages:

Scheduled→ In Progress→ Snagging→ Complete→ Paid

Scheduled — fitting date is booked in
In Progress — you're on-site, work is underway
Snagging — main work done, minor finishing touches needed
Complete — all work finished, customer happy
Paid — payment received in full

Revenue Tracking

The Jobs tab shows your revenue dashboard:

  • This week — total value of jobs completed this week
  • This month — running monthly revenue total
  • Outstanding — jobs marked Complete but not yet Paid

This gives you a real picture of your business performance without needing a spreadsheet.

Photo Reference Notes

Before every fitting, take photos on your phone camera: before (empty room), during (work in progress), and after (finished result).

The photos stay in your phone's camera roll. In the app, log a note against the job saying which photos you've taken — e.g., "Before/during/after photos taken, kitchen Karndean install." This reminds you which jobs have good photos ready for social media.

Pro Tip

Log every job — even small ones. The revenue dashboard shows your real monthly performance. A £300 hallway fitting is still £300 towards your monthly total.

Getting Google Reviews

Reviews are the single most powerful trust signal for a local business. This is where you close the gap.

⚠ New Image Carpets has 46 Google reviews at 4.9 stars. You currently have 0. This is the single biggest gap in your online presence — and the easiest one to fix.
How to Request a Review
  1. Go to the Reviews tab in the app
  2. Tap "Request Review" next to the customer's name
  3. The app opens WhatsApp with a pre-written message and your Google review link already included
  4. Tap Send in WhatsApp — that's it

The message is friendly, personal, and makes it easy for the customer. They just tap the link and leave a review.

When to Ask

The best moment to ask for a review is right after the customer says something like "I love it" or "It looks amazing". That's the peak of their happiness with the work.

Don't wait until the next day. Don't wait until you send the invoice. Ask in the moment, then send the WhatsApp link while you're still standing in their hallway.

Monthly Target
Target: 4 reviews per month
The app shows a progress bar for each month so you can see how you're tracking.

Not every customer will leave one. If you ask 6 customers a month, you'll likely get 4 reviews. Some months more, some less — the key is asking consistently.

Pro Tip

3 reviews a month for a year = 36 reviews. That puts you ahead of every independent flooring retailer in the area. Two years of this and you're untouchable on Google.

App Settings

Settings is where you control your business details, pricing, message templates, and backup. Most of this is already set up for you.

Business Details

Your business name, phone number, email, and address are pre-filled. If anything changes (new phone number, updated hours), edit it here. These details appear on your shared quotes.

Default Pricing (Price per m²)

The app uses price per square metre to calculate quote totals. This means:

  • Material cost — what you charge per m² for each flooring type (e.g., £35/m² for Karndean Knight Tile)
  • Fitting cost — your fitting rate per m² (e.g., £15/m²)
  • Extras — fixed prices for underlay, door bars, furniture moving, etc.

Set your standard rates here and they auto-fill every new quote. You can always override them on individual quotes for specific brands or products.

🄯 How to Find Your Google Review Link

▼

This is the link that customers tap to leave you a Google review. Here's how to find it:

  1. On your phone, open Google and search for "Whitburn Coastal Flooring"
  2. Find your Google Business Profile (the info card that appears with your address, hours, and photos)

Backup & Restore

All your data is stored on your phone. If you lose your phone or clear your browser data, the data is gone. That's why backup matters.

  1. Backup: Go to Settings, tap "Backup Data". This saves a file to your phone that contains all your customers, quotes, jobs, and settings
  2. Restore: If you ever need to restore, tap "Restore Data" and select your backup file
Do this weekly. One tap. Peace of mind. Set a reminder on your phone for every Friday afternoon.

Data & Privacy

A plain-English guide to keeping customer data safe and staying on the right side of UK law. This is simpler than you think.

What This Means for You

You're storing customer names, phone numbers, and addresses in this app. Under UK law (UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018), that makes you a data controller.

That sounds more serious than it is. It simply means you have a legal responsibility to look after that information properly. Here are the 4 things you need to do:

1. Register with the ICO (£40/year)

The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) is the UK's data privacy regulator. Almost every business that holds customer data needs to register. It costs £40 per year and takes about 10 minutes online.

  1. Go to ico.org.uk/registration
  2. Click "Register as a data controller"
  3. Answer the questions about your business (sole trader, number of staff, what data you hold)
  4. Pay £40 by card
  5. You'll get a registration number — keep this somewhere safe

Link: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/sme-web-hub/

2. Tell Customers You Store Their Data

When you take a customer's details, let them know you'll store their name, phone number, and address to manage their quote and fitting. You don't need a printed form for this — verbal is fine for now.

Something like: "I'll save your details in our system so I can manage your quote and stay in touch. We don't share your information with anyone."

3. Keep Your Phone Locked

Your customer data lives on your phone. A passcode or fingerprint lock is the single most important security measure you can take. If your phone is unlocked and it gets lost, anyone could access your customer details.

4. Back Up Your Data Regularly

If your phone breaks, gets stolen, or the data gets wiped, you need a backup. Use the app's Backup feature weekly (see the Settings tab). Store the backup file in a safe place — email it to yourself or save it to cloud storage.

Security Checklist

Tap each item once you've done it. Aim for all 8.

0 of 8 complete
  • Phone has a passcode or fingerprint/face lock enabled
  • Phone auto-locks after 1–2 minutes of inactivity
  • Registered with the ICO (or registration in progress)
  • App data backed up at least once
  • Backup file stored somewhere safe (email or cloud)
  • Weekly backup reminder set on phone
  • Verbal privacy notice given to customers when collecting details
  • Old customer data reviewed — delete anyone you no longer need
