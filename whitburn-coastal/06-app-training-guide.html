Welcome to Your App

The Flooring Business Manager v2 is a bespoke app built specifically for Whitburn Coastal Flooring. It handles your customers, quotes, jobs, reviews, analytics, and business settings — all from your phone. No login. No subscription. No monthly fees. It's yours.

✅ Your app is pre-loaded with your business details, 5 flooring brands with 22 product ranges, labour rates, sundry prices, and message templates — all ready to use out of the box.
First Launch: Security Notice

The first time you open the app, a security modal will appear. This is your GDPR notice — it explains that customer data is stored locally on your device, that you are the data controller, and that no data leaves your phone unless you choose to share it.

Tick the checkbox to confirm you understand, then tap "I Understand — Continue". This only appears once. It exists because storing customer names, phone numbers, and addresses makes you a data controller under UK law — the modal ensures you know that from day one.

Creating Quotes

This is the heart of the app. Build professional, room-by-room quotes using your full product catalogue with automatic pricing, then share branded quotes via WhatsApp in seconds. Each quote gets a unique number (e.g. WCF-001) and includes payment terms, T&Cs, and validity dates.

Full Quote Walkthrough
  1. Select a customer — go to the Quotes tab and tap the + button. Choose an existing customer from the dropdown, or tap "+ Add New Customer" to create one on the fly
  2. Set quote date and validity — the date defaults to today. Validity defaults to 30 days (configurable in Settings)
  3. Add a room — tap "+ Add Room" and select the room name (Kitchen, Living Room, Hallway, Bedroom 1, etc.)
  4. Enter dimensions — type the room length and width in metres. The app calculates the area automatically. For non-rectangular rooms, you can use the L-shape option or enter a manual m² override
  5. Choose Brand — select from the dropdown: Karndean, Alternative Flooring, iSENSE, Invictus, or Associated Weavers. Or type a custom product name for one-off items
  6. Choose Range — once you select a brand, the Range dropdown auto-populates with that brand's products. Each range shows its flooring type (LVT, Carpet) and fitting method (gluedown, click, stretch, herringbone)
  7. Price auto-fills — the trade price per m² fills in from your catalogue. You can override it for this specific quote if the customer needs a different price
  8. Labour rate auto-fills — based on the fitting method. E.g., carpet stretch-fit fills in your carpet rate, Karndean gluedown fills in your LVT gluedown rate. Editable per room
  9. Wastage auto-adjusts — standard rooms get 10% wastage. If you select a herringbone product, it automatically bumps to 15% (herringbone needs more material for pattern cuts)
  10. Add sundries — tick the relevant items: underlay, LVT underlay, gripper rods, door bars, furniture moving, old flooring uplift, subfloor prep, skirting refit, adhesive, scotia beading, or stair rods. Each has a default price and unit (per m², per room, each, per linear metre) that you can adjust
  11. Add more rooms — tap "+ Add Room" again to quote for additional rooms on the same job. Each room can have a different brand and product
  12. Review totals — the bottom of the quote shows: subtotal (materials + labour + sundries for all rooms), VAT (if enabled in Settings), and grand total. If you've set a margin percentage in Settings, that's applied too — but it's hidden from the customer view
  13. Save the quote — tap "Save Quote". The app assigns the next quote number (WCF-001, WCF-002, etc.)
  14. Share via WhatsApp — tap "Share Quote" to see a clean, white, professionally branded quote view. Tap "Send via WhatsApp" to share it with the customer

📐 L-Shape Rooms

▼

For L-shaped rooms, you have two options:

Option 1: Manual m² override — Switch the measure mode to "Manual" and enter the total area yourself. Useful if you've already calculated the area on-site or from architectural plans.

Option 2: Split into two rooms — Add the L-shape as two separate rooms (e.g., "Kitchen Main" and "Kitchen Extension") with standard rectangular dimensions for each. The quote totals combine them automatically.

The manual override is usually the cleanest approach — one room entry with the correct total area. Wastage still applies on top of whatever figure you enter.

🚾 Staircase Pricing

▼

Staircases are priced per step rather than per m². Your default rate is £20 per step (editable in Settings > Labour).

When adding a staircase room, enter the number of steps. The app multiplies steps by your per-step rate to calculate the labour cost. Material cost is still based on the m² area you enter.

A typical UK staircase has 13 steps, so at £20/step that's £260 for labour. Add this on top of materials and sundries (underlay, gripper rods, stair rods if applicable).

💰 Margin & Markup

▼

In Settings > Business, there's a Margin (%) field. This adds your profit margin on top of the quoted costs.

For example, if your quote totals £2,000 and you set a 10% margin, the customer sees £2,200. The margin is applied automatically and the customer never sees it broken out — it's rolled into the line item prices on the shared quote.

Set it to 0% if you don't want to use this feature. You can change it at any time and it only affects new quotes.

Data & Privacy

A plain-English guide to keeping customer data safe and staying on the right side of UK law. This is simpler than you think.

What This Means for You

You're storing customer names, phone numbers, and addresses in this app. Under UK law (UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018), that makes you a data controller.

That sounds more serious than it is. It simply means you have a legal responsibility to look after that information properly. The app has built-in tools to help you do this. Here are the key things you need to know and do:

First-Launch Security Modal

When you first open the app, a security modal appears explaining:

  • Customer data is stored locally on your device only
  • No data is sent to any server or third party
  • You are the data controller under UK GDPR
  • You should register with the ICO (see below)

You must tick the acknowledgement checkbox and tap "I Understand — Continue" to use the app. This only appears once.

1. Register with the ICO (£40/year)

The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) is the UK's data privacy regulator. Almost every business that holds customer data needs to register. It costs £40 per year and takes about 10 minutes online.

  1. Go to ico.org.uk/registration
  2. Click "Register as a data controller"
  3. Answer the questions about your business (sole trader, number of staff, what data you hold)
  4. Pay £40 by card
  5. You'll get a registration number — save this in Settings > Privacy in the app

Link: https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/sme-web-hub/

2. Tell Customers You Store Their Data

When you take a customer's details, let them know you'll store their name, phone number, and address to manage their quote and fitting. You don't need a printed form for this — verbal is fine for now.

Something like: "I'll save your details in our system so I can manage your quote and stay in touch. We don't share your information with anyone."

The app includes a full written privacy notice you can use for your showroom or future website (see below).

3. Keep Your Phone Locked

Your customer data lives on your phone. A passcode or fingerprint lock is the single most important security measure you can take. If your phone is unlocked and gets lost, anyone could access your customer details.

4. Back Up Your Data Regularly

If your phone breaks, gets stolen, or the data gets wiped, you need a backup. Go to Settings > Privacy and use the Backup feature weekly. Store the backup file in a safe place — email it to yourself or save it to cloud storage.

Do this weekly. One tap. Peace of mind. Set a reminder on your phone for every Friday afternoon.

📄 Per-Customer Data Export (GDPR Access Request)

▼

If a customer asks "What data do you hold on me?" — this is a Subject Access Request under GDPR. The app makes it easy to respond:

  1. Go to the Customers tab and find the customer
  2. Tap their name to open their profile
  3. Tap "Export Data"
  4. The app copies all their data to your clipboard — their contact details plus all linked quotes, jobs, and review records
  5. Paste it into an email or message and send it to them

You must respond to a data access request within 30 days. With this feature, you can do it in 30 seconds.

🗑 Data Deletion (Right to Erasure)

▼

If a customer asks you to delete their data, you can do so from their profile:

  1. Go to the Customers tab and find the customer
  2. Tap their name to open their profile
  3. Tap "Delete"
  4. Confirm the deletion in the dialog that appears

This permanently removes the customer record. Note: you may need to keep financial records (quotes, invoices) for up to 6 years for HMRC purposes. If someone requests deletion but you've done paid work for them, you can delete their personal details while retaining anonymised financial records.
