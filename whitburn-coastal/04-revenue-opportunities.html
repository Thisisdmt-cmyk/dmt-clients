3
Facebook Showroom Appointment Booking
£0
▼

Facebook has a built-in appointment booking feature that most small businesses never activate. Turn it on, set your available slots (matching your Tue–Sat hours), and add it as a call-to-action button on your page. Customers browsing your page at 9pm can book a Saturday showroom visit without needing to call. A booked appointment is ten times more likely to convert than a casual walk-in because the customer has already committed time to you.

Three WhatsApp messages, sent at three key moments, that turn every completed job into reviews, referrals, and repeat business. Day after fitting: a quick check-in (“How does the new floor look?”). Two weeks later: a gentle Google review request with a direct link. Six months later: a maintenance check-in that opens the door for aftercare services and referrals. The full message sequence with exact wording is ready to copy in the Assets tab.

5
Seasonal Campaign Calendar Launch
£0
▼

Spring refurbishment season is happening right now. People are redecorating after winter, preparing homes for sale, and upgrading rooms before summer entertaining. A simple Facebook post about spring flooring refreshes, timed to this week, costs nothing and taps into existing buying intent. The full 12-month campaign calendar in the Assets tab gives you a ready-made reason to post every single month, aligned to how people actually buy flooring throughout the year.

6
Pinterest-Driven Inspiration Funnel
£0 High Impact
▼

Pinterest is the single most powerful platform for flooring businesses that almost nobody in the North East is using. People searching “kitchen flooring ideas” or “herringbone LVT” on Pinterest have active purchase intent — they are planning a project, not casually browsing. Setting up an account and pinning your best project photos takes one afternoon and creates a permanent, searchable gallery that drives enquiries for years.

Landlord & Property Developer Package
Low Cost High Impact
▼

Sunderland has a strong rental market. Landlords need reliable, cost-effective flooring that looks professional and wears well — exactly what you supply. Create a dedicated landlord package with simplified pricing tiers for rental-grade flooring (hard-wearing carpet, LVT, and vinyl). 9
Flooring Maintenance Plan
Low Cost
▼

An annual “Flooring Health Check” — like an MOT for their floors. Annual carpet deep clean + LVT maintenance + joint inspection. This creates a guaranteed annual touchpoint with every past customer. During the inspection, you can identify areas that need attention, recommend upgrades for other rooms, and ask for referrals. Even if only 20% of past customers sign up, that is recurring revenue and a built-in referral opportunity every year. The branded Health Check card is in the Assets tab.

10
Referral Reward Scheme
Low Cost
▼

£50 credit per successful referral — deducted from the referee’s next order. Word of mouth is already your strongest channel. A formal reward scheme supercharges it. Hand every customer a referral card after installation (concept design in the Assets tab). When their friend or family member places an order and mentions the referral, the original customer gets £50 off their next purchase. On a £2,000+ average order, £50 is a tiny cost for a guaranteed warm lead.

11
“Design at Home” Premium Consultation Service
Low Cost High Impact
▼

Charge £75 for a premium home consultation — home visit, sample presentation, full room measurement, design advice, and a detailed quote. The £75 is deducted in full from any order placed. This achieves two things: it filters out time-wasters who book free home visits with no intention of buying, and it positions you as a premium, consultative service rather than just a shop. Customers who pay for a consultation convert at dramatically higher rates because they have already invested.

£0

Upload your strongest before-and-after photos, tag the brands used (Karndean, Margo Selby), and include your service area. This is a one-time setup that generates long-term inbound enquiries from exactly the kind of customer who values quality over price.
