WHAT EACH FIELD MEANS

Name
Customer's full name. First and last name so you can tell your John Smiths apart.
Phone
Their mobile number. This is essential — it's how the app sends WhatsApp reminders and review requests. No phone number = no reminders.
Car
Make, model, and year. For example: Ford Focus 2018. Helps you remember what they drive.
Registration
The car's registration plate. Handy for looking up MOT history and identifying vehicles.
MOT Due
This is the key field. Enter when their MOT expires and the app automatically adds them to the reminder system. This is how you generate repeat business.
Last Service
When they were last in. Gives you a picture of how often they visit.
Notes
Anything you want to remember. Access code for a gated property, "always wants a courtesy car", "wife's name is Sarah" — whatever helps you give great service.

FINDING AND CONTACTING CUSTOMERS

There's a search bar at the top of the Customers screen. Just start typing — it searches by name, phone number, car, or registration. You'll see results narrow down as you type.

Tap on any customer to open their details. Tap the Edit button to change anything — update their car, change the MOT date, add notes. Tap Save when you're done.

Open a customer's details and tap the phone icon. Your phone's dialler opens with their number ready to go. One tap and you're ringing them.

Open a customer's details and tap the WhatsApp icon. WhatsApp opens with a new chat to that customer, ready for you to type or send a message. No need to save their number in your phone contacts first.

Add every customer who comes through the door. Even if they only come for an MOT. In 6 months, your reminder system will be generating repeat business automatically.

YOUR DAILY ROUTINE

EVERY MORNING — 2 MINUTES

Open the MOT Reminders tab first thing. If anything is red or amber, send the reminder. That's it. Two minutes a day that keeps your workshop full.

2

3

Hit send in WhatsApp

After you send a reminder, tap "Mark as Contacted". This moves the customer from "needs reminder" to "waiting for reply". You'll know at a glance who you've already reached out to.

When a customer books in, tap "Mark as Booked". This completes the cycle. You reminded them, they replied, they're booked in. Job done.

DATA & PRIVACY

You're storing customer information digitally. Here's what that means and what you need to do to stay on the right side of the law.

WHAT THIS APP STORES

Customer names, phone numbers, car details, and job history. Under UK GDPR, this makes you a data controller. That sounds scary, but it's straightforward — here's what it means in plain English.

WHAT YOU NEED TO DO

Keep it secure
Lock your phone with a PIN or fingerprint. Don't let other people use the app unsupervised. This is the most important thing.
Only collect what you need
Name, phone, car, MOT date — that's all you need for the app to work. Don't store anything unnecessary like dates of birth or home addresses unless there's a good reason.
Delete if asked
If a customer asks you to remove their details, you must. Open their record, tap edit, and delete them. It's their right.
Tell customers
Let customers know you're keeping their details. A simple "We'll keep your details on file for MOT reminders, is that OK?" is enough. Most people say yes without thinking twice.

ICO REGISTRATION

Most small businesses that process customer data digitally need to pay the ICO data protection fee. It's £40 per year for small businesses.

Since you're storing customer personal data in this app, you almost certainly need to register.

1

Check if you need to register

Go to ico.org.uk/registration/self-assessment — it's a quick questionnaire that tells you if you need to pay.

2

Register and pay online

If you do need to register (you probably do), it takes 5 minutes. You pay £40 by card and you're done for the year.

PRACTICAL SECURITY STEPS

  • Lock your phone with a PIN, fingerprint, or face recognition
  • Don't let anyone else use the app unsupervised
  • Back up your data regularly (weekly is ideal)
  • If you get a new phone, transfer the backup file securely — don't leave copies lying around
  • Delete customer records when asked (open customer → edit → delete)
  • Check your ICO registration status at ico.org.uk

YOUR PRIVACY NOTICE

Here's a simple privacy notice you can read to customers, print out and put on the wall, or keep on hand if anyone asks. Tap the button to copy it.

Tyne & Wear Garage and Recovery keeps your name, phone number, and vehicle details to provide our services and send you MOT reminders. We store this data securely on our devices and don't share it with anyone. You can ask us to delete your details at any time.

Don't overthink this. Lock your phone, back up your data, and if anyone asks about their data, be upfront and honest. That's 90% of what data protection means for a small garage.
