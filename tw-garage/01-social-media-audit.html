10 Steps to Fix Everything

Work through these in order. The first five are critical and can all be done in a single afternoon. Click any step to expand the full instructions.

1

Rename Your Facebook Page

5 mins Critical
▶

Your page is currently called "Tyne and Wear Garage & tyre service's" — it needs to be Tyne & Wear Garage and Recovery.

  • Go to your Facebook Page → Settings → General → Name
  • Change it to: Tyne & Wear Garage and Recovery
  • Also update the username (the @handle) to something clean like @TyneWearGarage

Facebook may take up to 3 days to approve the change. Do this first so it's processing while you fix everything else.

2

Complete Your About Section

10 mins Critical
▶

Go to your Page → About → Edit each field. We've written the exact text for you in the Ready-Made Assets tab — just copy and paste it in.

  • Address: 6 Laygate, South Shields NE33 1SH
  • Phone: 07487 738527
  • Hours: Mon–Fri 9am–5pm, Sat 9am–1pm, Sun Closed
  • Category: Automotive Repair Shop
3

Claim Your Google Business Profile

15 mins Critical
▶

You have a 4.8-star rating on Google but the listing is unclaimed. That means you can't respond to reviews, update your hours, or add photos.

  • Search for your business on Google Maps
  • Click "Claim this business" or "Own this business?"
  • Follow Google's verification steps (usually a postcard or phone call)
  • Once verified, update hours, services, photos, and start responding to reviews
4

Update Profile Picture & Cover Photo

15 mins Critical
▶

Profile picture: Use a clear, simple logo or a sharp photo of your shop front. It must be recognisable at thumbnail size (170×170px minimum).

Cover photo: Use a wide photo (820×312px) showing your workshop, team, or a branded graphic that says what you do. Include "Vehicle Repairs | MOT | Tyres | Recovery" and your phone number if possible.

5

Set Up Auto-Reply Message

5 mins Critical
▶

When someone messages your page, they should get an instant reply — even if you're busy under a car.

  • Go to Page Settings → Messaging → Response Assistant
  • Turn on Instant Reply
  • We've written the exact auto-reply text in the Ready-Made Assets tab
6

Publish Your First Post Today

10 mins High
▶

Break the silence. Post something today — even a simple photo of the workshop with a caption. We've written five ready-to-post Facebook posts in the Ready-Made Assets tab. Pick one and post it now.

A photo taken on your phone of a real job is better than a polished stock image. People want to see the real business.

7

Ask 5 Happy Customers for Reviews

5 mins each High
▶

Your Google rating is excellent but only has 17 reviews — and they're all old. Fresh reviews dramatically improve local search ranking and customer trust.

  • After every job, send the customer a quick WhatsApp message asking for a review
  • We've written the exact messages in the Ready-Made Assets tab — just copy, paste, and send
  • Aim for 5 new reviews in the first fortnight
8

Commit to 2 Posts Per Week

20 mins/week High
▶

Consistency matters more than perfection. Two posts a week keeps your page alive and visible. A simple routine:

  • Tuesday: Before & after of a job, or a tip about car maintenance
  • Friday: Promo, customer shoutout, or behind-the-scenes photo

Take photos during the working day. Spend 5 minutes writing the post at the end of the day. Done.

9

Respond to Every Review & Comment

2 mins each Medium
▶

Reply to every Google and Facebook review, positive or negative. It shows you're active and that you care. Keep it brief and genuine — "Thanks John, glad we could help!" is perfect.

Same for Facebook comments. Every comment you reply to doubles the engagement on that post, which means Facebook shows it to more people.

10

Add Photos to Your Google Listing

10 mins Medium
▶

Once you've claimed your Google profile (Step 3), add at least 5 photos: your shopfront, the workshop inside, your team, and a couple of completed jobs. Listings with photos get 42% more requests for directions and 35% more clicks to websites.
