What Venues & Press Need

When Someone Googles You

A venue promoter, festival booker, journalist, or playlist curator will typically want all of this within 60 seconds:

  • One-line description — what do you sound like?
  • Short bio — who are you, what have you done?
  • Press photo — high-res, downloadable, landscape and portrait options
  • Music links — Spotify, Bandcamp, YouTube
  • Social links — Instagram, Facebook
  • Press quotes — even one meaningful endorsement
  • Contact details — booking email
  • Tech requirements — what do you need to play?

If any of this takes more than a minute to find, you lose bookings. It's that simple. An EPK puts it all in one place.

Band Bio (~200 words)

Lovely Assistant are a four-piece from Newcastle and Northumberland — Annie (keys, vocals), Kieran (bass, vocals), Dave (guitar), and Matt (drums). Formed in 2017 and working from a community centre, they make music they describe as "defiantly without genre." Their sound draws on the torch songs of the 1950s, the warmth of Laurel Canyon, and the restless spirit of artists like Prefab Sprout, Arthur Russell, Angel Olsen, and J.J. Cale. It's been called New Lounge, spartan-jazz, and indie folk. None of those labels quite fit, which is probably the point. Their second album "Fuck Love" (2025), produced by Will Thorneycroft with select tracks executive produced by Thom Lewis at Blank Studio, was recorded at a remote farmhouse in the Midlands and mastered by J.J. Golden in California. It was funded by Help Musicians. Their debut "Greetings from the Pre-wave" (2020) was released on limited 12" vinyl, also produced by Thorneycroft. A live album follows in spring 2026. Praised by Guy Garvey and supported by Generator NE and Glasshouse ICM, Lovely Assistant tour the UK's village halls, arts centres, and independent venues — the kind of places where the music and the room feel like they belong together. Sync representation: Chelsea Wood Booking: lovelyassistantmusic@gmail.com
