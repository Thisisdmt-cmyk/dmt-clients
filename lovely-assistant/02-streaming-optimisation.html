Prioritised Opportunities

High Priority

1. Spotify for Artists Optimisation

If you haven't already claimed and fully set up your Spotify for Artists profile, that's step one. If you have, there's likely more you can do:

  • Bio — write a proper one (there's a ready-to-paste version in the Assets tab). This appears on your profile and in search results.
  • Artist Pick — pin your latest single or upcoming live album to the top of your profile. Change this with every release.
  • Canvas — those 8-second looping videos that play behind tracks. A simple clip of a live performance works perfectly. Tracks with Canvas get more saves.
  • Gallery images — upload proper press photos and live shots. This populates your profile and makes it look active.
  • Header image — make sure it's current and high resolution.
High Priority

2. Live Album as Growth Catalyst

The live album is your biggest streaming opportunity in 2026. Here's how to use it:

  • Submit to Spotify editorial playlists via Spotify for Artists — you can pitch one unreleased track at least 7 days before release. Write a compelling pitch (template in Assets tab).
  • Set up a pre-save campaign — DistroKid, TuneCore, or your distributor likely supports this. Share the pre-save link everywhere.
  • Release on a Friday — Spotify's editorial playlists refresh on Fridays. Releasing on any other day means missing a full week of potential playlist placement.
  • Don't release all tracks at once as singles beforehand — save at least 60-70% of the album as fresh tracks. Spotify's algorithm favours new content.
High Priority

3. Playlist Pitching (Independent Curators)

Beyond Spotify editorial, there are thousands of independent playlist curators. These are real people who run playlists because they love music:

  • Find playlists your style fits — search Spotify for playlists with keywords like "indie folk", "lounge", "chamber pop", "UK indie". Look for playlists with 200-5,000 followers (they're more likely to add emerging artists).
  • Reach out directly — many curators list contact info in the playlist description. A short, genuine email works (template in Assets tab).
  • SubmitHub — a paid platform (~$1-2 per submission) where you pitch to curators directly. Worth it for a live album release.
  • Pitch your strongest tracks first — Passengers Reminded (3,806 plays) is your proven performer.
Medium Priority

4. Bandcamp Fridays & Direct Sales

Bandcamp is where your most dedicated fans live. For the live album:

  • Time the Bandcamp release to a Bandcamp Friday (first Friday of each month) when Bandcamp waives their revenue share. Fans know about these days.
  • Offer exclusive formats — name-your-price digital, limited edition physical if you can swing it.
  • Write a proper album description (template in Assets tab) — tell the story of the recording.
  • Update your Bandcamp bio to mention the live album and link to your other platforms.
Medium Priority

5. YouTube Live Sessions

You've already got live videos on YouTube. You might want to consider making this a regular thing:

  • Film one live session video per single/album release — even a phone recording in the community centre with decent audio.
  • Optimise titles and descriptions for search — include the song name, "Lovely Assistant", "live", and genre terms.
  • Create a playlist on your channel — "Live Sessions" or "Live at..." grouping. This keeps people watching.
  • YouTube Shorts — crop the best 30-60 seconds of any live video into a Short. These get algorithmic push.
When You're Ready

6. Apple Music Optimisation

Apple Music has a significant UK listener base, particularly among people who buy music (not just stream). Make sure your Apple Music for Artists profile is claimed, bio is filled in (template in Assets tab), and your discography is complete.
