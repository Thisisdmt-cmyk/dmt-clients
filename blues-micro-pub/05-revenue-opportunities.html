12 Revenue Opportunities

Each opportunity includes estimated revenue, difficulty rating, and practical implementation notes. Click any card to expand the full detail.

1
Merchandise
CAMRA Pub of the Year branded goods
£200 - £500/mo
Easy
▼

The CAMRA Pub of the Year win makes this a no-brainer. People want a memento from visiting Britain's best pub. Every visitor is a potential merch customer.

Product ideas and pricing:

  • Branded pint glasses — £8-12 each
  • "I drank at Britain's best pub" t-shirts — £15-20
  • Tote bags with Blue's branding — £8-10
  • Beer mats / coasters — sell packs of 4 for £5
  • Branded bottle opener keyrings — £5
Implementation Path

Design artwork → Source print supplier → Order initial stock → Display in pub → Add to Square online shop. Start with t-shirts and glasses as they have the highest margins and demand.

2
Gift Cards & Vouchers
"A round at Britain's best pub"
£100 - £300/mo
Easy
▼

Position gift cards as the perfect present: "A round at Britain's best pub." Offer £10, £20, and £50 denominations, available in the pub and online.

Square already supports digital gift cards — this is a matter of activating the feature and promoting it.

Peak selling periods:

  • Christmas — biggest gift-buying season
  • Father's Day — "buy dad a round at the best pub in Britain"
  • Birthdays — year-round steady seller
  • Valentine's Day — couples' experience gift
Implementation Path

Activate Square digital gift cards → Design branded physical card for in-pub sales → Create promotional signage → Push on social media before peak periods. Virtually zero setup cost.

3
Tasting Events
Premium monthly ticketed experiences
£200 - £400/mo
Medium
▼

Monthly premium ticketed events that attract enthusiasts and generate higher spend per head than a regular session. The Whisky Club already proves this model works.

Event concepts:

  • Cider tasting masterclass — 6 ciders, £15-20pp, max 20 people
  • Whisky evening — premium malts, £25-30pp, max 15 people
  • Meet the Brewer nights — local brewery partnership, £10-15pp
  • Seasonal specials — Christmas ales, summer ciders, harvest beers
Implementation Path

Choose first event type → Set date and capacity → Create event listing → Sell tickets via Square or Eventbrite → Promote via social media and newsletter → Run event → Collect feedback and repeat.

4
Beer Festival
Annual "Blues Fest" weekend celebration
£2,000 - £5,000/yr
Hard
▼

An annual weekend celebration that becomes a calendar highlight. Think of it as the flagship event that generates buzz, press coverage, and significant revenue in a concentrated period.

Blues Fest concept:

  • 20+ guest ales and ciders from across the UK
  • Live music in the beer garden
  • Food stalls — partner with local street food vendors
  • Ticketed entry: £5-10, includes first pint + commemorative glass
  • Timing: Late summer when the beer garden is at its best
Implementation Path

Plan 3-4 months ahead → Book breweries and musicians → Arrange food partners → Apply for temporary events licence if needed → Sell advance tickets → Promote heavily through all channels. Consider starting smaller in year one and growing.

5
Collaboration Brews
Co-branded beers with local breweries
Variable
Medium
▼

Partner with North East breweries to create exclusive beers that can only be found at Blue's. This creates a unique selling point and a reason for beer enthusiasts to visit.

Collaboration ideas:

  • "Blue's Best" — a signature house ale brewed to your specification
  • "Whitburn Gold" — a golden ale celebrating the village
  • Sold exclusively at Blue's = unique selling point and destination driver
  • Bottles and cans for takeaway = additional revenue stream
Implementation Path

Approach 2-3 local breweries → Agree on beer style and branding → Brew a small batch → Launch with a "Meet the Brewer" evening → Sell on tap and in bottles/cans for takeaway. The CAMRA win makes Blue's an attractive partner for any brewery.

6
Food Expansion
Beyond pies — no kitchen required
£300 - £600/mo
Medium
▼

Currently offering pies on Thursday and a free cheeseboard on Sunday. The opportunity is to expand the food offering without investing in a commercial kitchen by partnering with local suppliers.

Expansion opportunities:

  • Partner with a local baker for a daily pie offering (not just Thursday)
  • Charcuterie boards — £8-12, available daily
  • Scotch eggs and pork pies from local suppliers
  • Cheese boards available to purchase (not just free on Sunday)
Implementation Path

Identify local food suppliers → Agree on wholesale pricing and delivery schedule → Create a simple bar snacks menu → Display on chalkboard or printed menu → Track what sells and adjust. Food keeps people in the pub longer and increases spend per visit.

7
Private Experiences
Intimate venue for special occasions
£100 - £300/mo
Easy
▼

A small venue is actually an advantage for private bookings — it feels exclusive and intimate. Position the pub (or the upstairs games room) as a premium private hire space.

Booking types:

  • Birthday celebrations — minimum spend requirement, reserved area
  • Work leaving dos and retirement parties
  • Anniversary celebrations
  • Corporate team building — craft beer tasting + cheese pairing experience
Implementation Path

Define available times and spaces → Set minimum spend thresholds → Create a simple booking enquiry process → Add "Private Hire" page to website → Promote on social media and local community groups.

8
Online Shop
Square-powered e-commerce
£100 - £200/mo
Easy
▼

Square already supports online selling — this is about activating the feature and stocking it with desirable products. The online shop extends your reach far beyond Whitburn.

Products to sell online:

  • Digital gift cards — instant delivery, zero fulfilment cost
  • Merchandise — t-shirts, glasses, keyrings shipped to customers

Activate Square Online → Add gift cards first (instant win, no shipping) → Add merchandise with photos and descriptions → Set up shipping rates → Promote on social media and in the pub. Start simple, grow from there.

9
Loyalty Programme
Digital stamp card via Square Loyalty
Drives Retention
Easy
▼

A digital loyalty programme that rewards regular customers and collects valuable data. Square Loyalty is a free feature already built into the system you use.

Programme structure:

  • Buy 10 pints, get 1 free — simple and easy to understand
  • Cask Club members get double stamps — rewards your best customers

10
Tourism Partnerships
Beer trails, coastal walks, local accommodation
Long-term Growth
Medium
▼

As CAMRA Pub of the Year, Blue's is now a destination. Leverage this by integrating with local tourism infrastructure to capture visitors who are already in the area.

Partnership opportunities:

Monthly Newsletter Template
▼

"The Blues Brief" — your monthly newsletter structure. Use this as a template in Mailchimp or any email platform.

Section 1: What's On This Month

List all upcoming events, themed nights, and specials for the coming month. Include dates, times, and booking links. This is your primary revenue-driving section.

Section 2: Beer of the Month

Spotlight one ale or cider currently on tap. Include tasting notes, the brewery story, and why you chose it. Makes readers want to visit and try it before it's gone.

Section 3: Event Recap

A brief look back at last month's highlights. Include 1-2 photos if possible. Creates FOMO for those who missed out and validates those who attended.

Section 4: Exclusive Offer

A subscriber-only deal. Could be early-bird event access, a discount code for the online shop, or a free half-pint with a meal. This is what makes people stay subscribed.

Section 5: From the Team

A personal note from the pub. What you're excited about, a thank you to customers, or a behind-the-scenes story. Keeps it human and builds community.

Sending schedule: First week of each month. Keep it concise — aim for 3-5 minutes reading time. Free via Mailchimp up to 500 subscribers.

25th January

Valentine's Couples' Tasting
14th February

An intimate evening for couples. 4 beers paired with 4 artisan cheeses, served as a shared board. £20pp / £35 per couple. Candlelit setting. Max 10 couples.

Father's Day Gift & Pint
Third Sunday in June

Pre-packaged gift sets: branded glass + gift card + bottle opener in a gift box (£25-30). Promote online 3 weeks ahead. In-pub, offer a "Dad's First Pint Free" with any gift set purchase.

Summer Beer Garden Sessions
July - August (weekends)

Extended weekend hours in the beer garden. Rotating guest ciders. Optional BBQ partnership with a local vendor. Live acoustic music on Saturdays. No ticket needed — just show up.

Halloween Dark Ales Night
31st October

All taps go dark: stouts, porters, dark fruit ciders, and black IPAs. Themed decorations. "Trick or Treat" — mystery half-pint pours at a set price. Fancy dress encouraged.

12 Ales of Christmas
13th - 24th December

A different featured guest ale each day for 12 days. Tasting card — collect all 12 for a reward (free pint or merch discount). Daily reveals on social media. Creates a reason to visit every day.
