A Note on Financial Data

Everything below is a qualitative assessment based on publicly visible information: your website, social media, review platforms, news coverage, and CAMRA listings. Revenue estimates in Tab 2 are ranges based on comparable UK micropub benchmarks and should be validated against your actual trading data.

Your Local Market — Whitburn village has ~7,170 residents with a median age of 46 and 44% retired — a perfect micropub demographic. The village core is predominantly outright homeowners in detached properties, indicating high disposable income. Beyond residents, Souter Lighthouse and Whitburn Coastal Park attract ~70,000 visits per year, and five established walking routes pass through the area. The Great North Run (60,000 runners, September) and Bents Park Sunday Concerts (up to 20,000 per week, July–August) create significant seasonal footfall peaks. A pending planning application for 205 new homes off Mill Lane would further grow the local customer base.

Revenue Estimates: An Honest Caveat

All revenue ranges below are estimates based on comparable UK micropub benchmarks and publicly reported figures from similar venues. They are not projections or promises. Your actual results will depend on your pricing, location footfall, execution, and factors we can't see from the outside. Treat these as informed starting points for your own planning.

1
Gift Cards & Vouchers
Physical and digital gift cards for your regulars to share
£1,500 – £4,000 /yr
Low Effort
▾
What It Is
Branded gift cards in fixed denominations (£10, £20, £50) sold at the bar and promoted on Instagram. Redeemable against any drink or event.
Setup Required
Print gift cards (design provided in Tab 3), set up a tracking system (simple spreadsheet or Square's built-in gift card feature), announce on Instagram.
Revenue Driver
Gift cards are pre-paid revenue. Industry estimates suggest recipients often spend above the card value. A proportion of gift cards go unredeemed, contributing to margin.
Why This Suits Blues Specifically

You already use Square for card-only payments, which has built-in gift card functionality. Your 2,200 Instagram followers are a ready audience to announce gift cards, especially around Christmas, Valentine's Day, and Father's Day. Your strong local reputation means people already want to recommend you — a gift card makes that recommendation tangible.

Revenue range based on comparable micropubs with 100-200 regular customers reporting annual gift card sales. Your results will vary based on promotion effort and seasonal timing.

2
Branded Merchandise
T-shirts, pint glasses, tote bags and bottle openers
£2,000 – £6,000 /yr
Medium Effort
▾
What It Is
A small range of Blues-branded products: t-shirts, pint glasses, tote bags, and bottle openers. Sold at the bar and promoted through Instagram stories and posts.
Setup Required
Source a local print supplier, decide on initial range (start with 2-3 items), invest in first batch stock (roughly £300-£600), create a small display area at the bar.
Revenue Driver
Margins on branded merch can be strong, typically ranging from 50-70% depending on product and volume. A £20 t-shirt costs £6-8 to produce. Every item sold is also a walking advertisement for Blues.
Why This Suits Blues Specifically

Your Instagram following (2,200) proves people identify with your brand. The CAMRA Branch POTY 2026 award adds a "collector" angle — limited-run award-winner merch. Your card-only setup simplifies payment tracking. The pricing calculator in Tab 3 will help you work out margins for each item.

Revenue range based on micropubs and craft beer bars reporting merch sales of £40-120/week. Lower end assumes bar-only sales; upper end assumes Instagram promotion driving orders.

3
Tasting Events & Experiences
Ticketed beer, cider and whisky tasting sessions
£3,000 – £8,000 /yr
Medium Effort
▾
What It Is
Structured tasting events: guided beer flights, cider masterclasses, whisky evenings. Ticketed at £15-£35 per head. Groups of 8-20 in the main bar or upstairs Games Room.
Setup Required
Create an event format and tasting notes, set up booking (form provided in Tab 3), promote via Instagram and in-venue signage. You already run Whisky Club — this expands that proven model.
Revenue Driver
Pre-sold tickets guarantee revenue before the event. Attendees typically buy additional drinks. Group bookings for birthdays, stag/hen dos, and corporate outings can command premium pricing.
Why This Suits Blues Specifically

You already run Whisky Club monthly — this proves you can deliver curated tasting experiences. Your 19 rotating lines (cask + keg + cider) give you natural variety for beer flights without extra stock. The Games Room provides a semi-private space for groups. Your 4.9 Google rating makes you a credible "experience" venue, not just a pub.

Revenue range assumes 2-4 ticketed events per month at £15-£30/head with 8-15 attendees. Based on reported figures from comparable micropub tasting events across the UK.

4
Games Room Private Hire
Monetise your upstairs space for private parties and events
£1,000 – £3,000 /yr
Low Effort
▾
What It Is
Offer the upstairs Games Room for private hire: birthday parties, small corporate gatherings, quiz nights, or club meetings. Charge a flat hire fee or set a minimum bar spend.
Setup Required
Decide on hire terms (flat fee vs minimum spend), create a simple booking process, add to your website and Instagram bio. Minimal investment — the space already exists.
Revenue Driver
Private groups typically spend more per head than walk-in customers. A £50-£100 hire fee or £150 minimum spend guarantees revenue on quieter evenings.
Why This Suits Blues Specifically

You already have the space — it's currently a free amenity. Whitburn is a residential village where venue options for small private events are limited. A separate upstairs room gives privacy without disrupting the main bar trade. This requires almost no additional labour or investment.

Revenue range assumes 2-6 private bookings per month at £50-£150 each. Lower end reflects occasional use; upper end reflects active promotion and weekend bookings.

5
Loyalty Programme
Stamp cards to reward repeat visits and increase frequency
Indirect
Low Effort
▾
What It Is
A simple stamp card: 10 visits earns a free pint. Physical cards kept by the customer, stamped at each purchase. Low-tech, high-impact.
Setup Required
Print loyalty cards (design provided in Tab 3), buy a branded stamp, brief staff on the process. Total setup cost under £50.
Revenue Driver
Loyalty programmes are widely reported to increase visit frequency in hospitality. The cost of one free pint per 10 visits is negligible compared to the additional revenue from those 10 visits.
Why This Suits Blues Specifically

As a village micropub, your regulars are your business. A loyalty card gives them one more reason to choose Blues over staying in or going elsewhere. It also creates a talking point — people show their nearly-full cards to friends. The card design in Tab 3 is print-ready and on-brand.

Revenue impact is indirect: increased visit frequency rather than a new revenue line. Loyalty programmes are widely reported to increase repeat visits for hospitality venues, though the impact varies widely.

Indirect

A weekly or fortnightly email newsletter: this week's beers on tap, upcoming events, a staff pick, community news. Sent via Mailchimp (free tier covers up to 500 subscribers).

Your Instagram proves you can create content consistently. A newsletter repurposes that effort into a channel no algorithm can throttle. The template in Tab 3 is ready to copy into Mailchimp.

Revenue impact is indirect: newsletters drive event bookings, gift card purchases, and repeat visits. Quantifying the exact impact requires tracking, but the setup cost is zero (Mailchimp free tier).

Four anchor events across the year — one per season — designed to create anticipation, drive social media content, and give people a reason to visit beyond their usual routine. Full concepts in Tab 3.

8
Google Review Growth
Turn your 4.9-star rating into a systematic footfall engine
Indirect
Low Effort
▾
What It Is
A deliberate strategy to grow Google reviews from 119 towards 300+. Print a QR code linking to your Google review page. Place it on tables, near the bar, and on the back of loyalty cards. Mention it on Instagram periodically.
Setup Required
Generate your Google review link, create a QR code (free tools available), print small table cards. Total cost: under £20. Five minutes of effort.
Revenue Driver
Google reviews directly influence search ranking and click-through rates. Moving from 119 to 300+ reviews while maintaining 4.9 stars makes you the obvious choice for anyone searching "pub near me" in the Whitburn/South Shields area.
Why This Suits Blues Specifically

You already have a 4.9-star rating — that's the hard part done. You just need volume. For comparison, Tamworth Tap has 619 reviews at 4.8 stars. If Blues reached 300 reviews at 4.9 stars, you'd have one of the strongest Google profiles of any micropub in the North East. Every new review is free, permanent marketing that works 24/7.

Revenue impact is indirect and long-term: higher Google visibility drives discovery by new customers. Exact footfall attribution to reviews is difficult to measure, but the correlation between review volume and local search ranking is well-documented.

4 Seasonal Event Concepts

One event per season, each as a complete mini-brief: name, description, menu suggestions, pricing, marketing, and capacity.

Spring — April / May
The Cider Awakening
A celebration of real cider to coincide with the start of the outdoor drinking season. You won CAMRA Cider Pub of the Year — this event owns that title.
Format
Guided cider tasting flight (5 ciders) with tasting notes and a cheese board pairing. 2-hour session, Saturday afternoon.
Suggested Menu
  • 5 x 1/3 pint cider samples (mix of sweet, dry, heritage)
  • Regional cheese board (pair each cheese with a cider)
  • Printed tasting notes card (keepsake)
Pricing
£18-£22 per person. Includes flight + cheese board. Additional pints at bar prices.
Marketing
Lead with your CAMRA Cider POTY award. Instagram countdown stories. Share on local Whitburn community pages. Offer early-bird pricing for newsletter subscribers.
Capacity
15-20 people per session. Run 2 sessions if demand warrants it.
Summer — July / August
Blues & Brews Festival
A weekend mini beer festival showcasing local and regional breweries. Pair with live acoustic music (blues, naturally) for a signature annual event.
Format
Friday evening through Sunday afternoon. 12-16 guest beers and ciders beyond the usual rotation. Live acoustic sets Friday and Saturday evenings.
Suggested Menu
  • 12-16 guest ales and ciders on stillage or extra keg lines
  • Festival pie specials (collaborate with your Pie & Pint supplier)
  • Souvenir festival pint glass (branded, sold at £5)
Pricing
Free entry. Drinks at normal prices. Festival glass available for £5. Musicians paid a flat fee or in-kind (tab).
Marketing
Announce brewery line-up over 4 weeks via Instagram stories (one reveal per day). Create a Facebook event. Submit to CAMRA event listings. Posters in Whitburn village.
Capacity
Full venue capacity across the weekend. Use Games Room as overflow. Target 80-120 unique visitors over 3 days.
Autumn — October
Dark & Stormy Night
An evening of dark beers, stouts, porters, and whisky — timed around Halloween for atmosphere. Dim the lights, light some candles, lean into the cosy micropub setting.
Format
One-night ticketed event, Friday or Saturday nearest Halloween. Dark beer flight paired with a whisky dram. Atmospheric setting with low lighting and candles.
Suggested Menu
  • 4 x dark beer samples (stout, porter, dark mild, imperial)
  • 1 x whisky dram (tie into Whisky Club branding)
  • Dark chocolate and salted caramel board
Pricing
£22-£28 per person. Includes beer flight, whisky dram, and tasting board. Premium positioning justified by the experience.
Marketing
Moody Instagram photography (dark beers, candlelight). Sell as a "date night" or "mates' night" experience. Limit tickets to create scarcity.
Capacity
Intimate: 15-25 people maximum. Scarcity drives demand and justifies premium pricing.
Winter — December
12 Beers of Christmas
A 12-day countdown featuring a different special beer each day in the lead-up to Christmas. Drives daily footfall during the busiest pub season and creates daily Instagram content.
Format
12 consecutive days (e.g. 13th-24th December). Each day, reveal and tap a different special or rare beer. One "advent" beer per day alongside regular rotation.
Suggested Menu
  • 12 x special/seasonal beers (one per day, sourced in advance)
  • Mince pies or mulled cider on selected evenings
  • Gift card promotion (perfect timing for last-minute gifts)
Pricing
No ticket required. Special beers at normal or slight premium pricing. Revenue comes from increased footfall (people visit specifically to try the daily special).
Marketing
Daily "reveal" on Instagram Stories. Create anticipation with a countdown graphic. Newsletter subscribers get the full list in advance as a perk. Cross-promote gift cards heavily.
Capacity
Normal capacity. The appeal is daily regulars returning plus curious new visitors. Target 20-30% uplift in daily covers during the 12-day period.
