What Makes Them Work

Decoding the Tamworth formula

  1. They tell their story everywhere — 16th century building, castle views, sustainability, local brewing. Every channel reinforces the same narrative.
  2. CAMRA badges are deployed as marketing assets — on profile photos, cover photos, website hero, window signage. They never let you forget.
  3. Multi-platform presence means they reach beer tourists, locals, and casual browsers simultaneously.
  4. The newsletter keeps customers engaged between visits, building repeat trade and loyalty.
  5. The virtual tour lets people "visit" before they visit — converting curious browsers into actual footfall.
  6. Events are promoted across all channels simultaneously, maximising reach for every booking.
  7. Food offering (bar snacks) adds revenue and extends dwell time per customer.
  8. Owning a brewery gives them a unique story and product exclusivity that competitors can't replicate.
  9. Their sustainability narrative resonates with modern, conscious consumers.
  10. Community feel with professional execution — they feel authentic while looking polished.

Where Blues Can Win

Their weaknesses are your opportunities

  • Location advantage: Tamworth is a busy town centre. Blues is in Whitburn — a coastal village with charm, character, and a story that writes itself.
  • Intimacy wins: Tamworth is a multi-floor venue. Blues has the intimate village micropub feel that people actually seek out and remember.
  • The crown has moved: Tamworth held the title. Blues holds it NOW (2026). The current champion always has the spotlight.
  • Double champion: Blues has CAMRA Cider Pub of the Year too. Tamworth doesn't. That's two national titles.
  • Events diversity: Running club, whisky club, free cheeseboard Sundays, pie nights — Blues' events programme is more creative and varied.
  • Authentic to the core: Cal and Alice ARE the pub. It's personal, genuine, and real. People connect with people, not brands.
