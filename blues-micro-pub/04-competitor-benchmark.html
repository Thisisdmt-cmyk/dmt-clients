What Makes Them Work

The formula behind the benchmark

  1. Own brewery gives them a unique story and product exclusivity that other micropubs cannot replicate.
  2. 16th century building with medieval cellars and castle views — every channel reinforces the same narrative.
  3. Awards as assets — CAMRA badges are deployed on every profile photo, cover image, website hero, and window.
  4. Professional website with live beer board, virtual tour, events calendar, and newsletter signup.
  5. "The Tap Times" newsletter keeps customers engaged between visits, building repeat trade.
  6. Beer garden overlooking Tamworth Castle gives them an iconic visual that photographs beautifully.
  7. Multi-platform presence means they reach beer tourists, locals, and browsers simultaneously.
🎯

What They Don't Do Well

Even the best have blind spots

Instagram: 33 posts. That's it. The three-time CAMRA National Pub of the Year has posted 33 times on Instagram. Blues has posted 1,745 times — that's 53x the content volume. Tamworth has the awards; Blues has the content engine.
Instagram followers: Tamworth has 1,792 followers. Blues has 2,200 — a 23% lead over the three-time national champion. Blues wins this metric outright.
Google rating: Tamworth scores 4.8 stars. Blues scores 4.9 stars. Higher quality rating, even if the review volume needs work.

The takeaway: Tamworth is brilliant at structure, website, reviews, and awards leverage. But they are barely trying on Instagram — the platform where Blues already dominates. This isn't just a gap to close; it's proof that Blues' content instincts are genuinely world-class.

Blues Already Leads on Three Metrics

Before looking at the gaps, recognise this: Blues outperforms the three-time CAMRA National Pub of the Year on Instagram followers, Instagram content, and Google rating. For a two-person micropub, that's remarkable.

Quick Wins

Weeks 1–4 • Claim, optimise, start the review push
  • ✓
    Claim Untappd venue — the listing exists but is unverified. Claim it so beers and check-ins link back to Blues
  • ✓
    Optimise Google Business Profile — update description, add CAMRA award mention, upload 10+ quality photos, verify hours
  • ✓
    Print Google review QR code cards — place on bar, hand out with every tab. Make it effortless for customers to review
  • ✓
    Start TripAdvisor push — optimise the listing with photos and full description. Ask regulars to review (8 reviews to 30 is realistic)
  • ✓
    Update Instagram bio — add "CAMRA Branch Pub of the Year 2026" and "Cider POTY 2026" to bio
  • ✓
    Pin CAMRA award celebration post on Instagram and Facebook
  • ✓
    Respond to every existing Google review — go through all 119 and reply to any unanswered ones
🎯 End of Phase 1: Google reviews to 150+, TripAdvisor reviews to 25+, Untappd claimed, all profiles updated with CAMRA awards
2

Content Rhythm

Weeks 5–8 • Formalise what works, expand reach
  • ✓
    Formalise Instagram excellence — Blues already posts brilliantly. Add a weekly rhythm: 3 feed posts + daily stories when open
  • ✓
    Start Facebook consistency — cross-post best Instagram content to Facebook. Aim for 3 posts per week
  • ✓
    Create Facebook Events for every recurring session — whisky club, pie & pint Thursday, running club, cheeseboard Sunday
  • ✓
    Build a content library — photograph events, pours, the village, the customers (with permission). Bank content for quiet days
  • ✓
    Respond to every new review on Google and TripAdvisor within 48 hours — make it a habit, not a task
  • ✓
    Continue review generation push — keep the QR cards prominent, verbally ask happy customers
🎯 End of Phase 2: Google reviews to 175+, TripAdvisor to 35+, Facebook posting consistently 3x/week, Instagram rhythm formalised
3

Growth

Weeks 9–12 • Expand, experiment, build the foundation for what's next
  • ✓
    Explore TikTok — create an account. Post 2–3 simple videos: a pour, a pub tour, a village walk. See what sticks before committing
  • ✓
    Consider a newsletter — Mailchimp free tier. Monthly "Blues Brief" with what's coming up, new beers, events. Start collecting emails with a signup sheet at the bar
  • ✓
    Review generation final push — target 200 Google reviews by end of 90 days. Continue TripAdvisor momentum
  • ✓
    Cross-promote with local businesses — tag and collaborate with Whitburn village businesses on social
  • ✓
    Audit what's working — review Instagram insights, Google analytics, and review growth. Double down on what works
  • ✓
    Plan next steps — website upgrade? Untappd beer board? Merchandise? Decide based on actual data, not guesswork
🎯 End of Phase 3: Google reviews to 200+, TripAdvisor to 20–25, TikTok tested, newsletter explored, content rhythm sustainable long-term
