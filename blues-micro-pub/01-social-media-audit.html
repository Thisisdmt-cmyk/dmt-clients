Your 10-Step Action Plan

Prioritised steps to turn your CAMRA win into social media momentum.

1

Facebook Page Health Check Urgent

Ensure your Facebook page is set to Public so customers can actually find you. Complete every info field: address, opening hours, phone, website. Add your CAMRA Pub of the Year 2026 award to the bio and About section. Update the cover photo to something that screams "award-winning pub." This takes 20 minutes and is the single most important thing you can do today.

2

Facebook Content Strategy Urgent

Post 4–5 times per week minimum. Your content pillars: beer board updates (what's on tap), event announcements (Cask Club, Pie Night, Whisky Club), behind-the-scenes with Cal & Alice, customer stories, and community posts about Whitburn village. Consistency is everything — a quiet Facebook page tells people you're closed.

3

Pin the CAMRA Win Urgent

Create a pinned post celebrating your Pub of the Year 2026 win. This is your single biggest marketing asset — possibly ever. Pin it to the top of Facebook, make it your Instagram highlight, put it in every bio. When someone visits your page, this is the first thing they should see. Use the ready-made post from Tab 3.

4

Instagram Content Calendar Urgent

Daily Instagram Stories showing what's on tap. Three feed posts per week minimum. Start creating Reels — short clips of cask ales being poured, your six ciders on the bar, the snug room, the beer garden. Reels are how Instagram pushes your content to new audiences. One good Reel can reach 10x your follower count.

5

Launch TikTok Urgent

Set up @bluesmicropub on TikTok today. Your first video: "We just won CAMRA Pub of the Year 2026. Here's why." Walk through the pub. Show the cask ales. Show the ciders. Show the beer garden. Keep it short, authentic, and proud. You don't need fancy editing — authenticity wins on TikTok. Cross-post every TikTok to Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts.

6

Google Reviews Push This Month

You have 4.9 stars from 119 reviews — outstanding quality, but the volume needs to grow. Target: 250+ reviews within 6 months. Print a small card or table tent with a QR code linking to your Google review page. Ask every happy customer. Mention it at the end of events. Every review builds your visibility in local search results.

7

TripAdvisor Setup This Month

Create or claim your TripAdvisor listing. Beer tourists — especially international visitors — use TripAdvisor to find pubs. As CAMRA Pub of the Year, you'll attract visitors from across the UK and beyond. You must be discoverable where they're looking. Add photos, opening hours, and your CAMRA credentials.

8

Cross-Platform Hashtag Strategy This Month

Use a consistent set of hashtags across every post on every platform. Your core set: #BluesMicroPub #WhitburnVillage #CAMRAPubOfTheYear #RealAle #CraftCider #Micropub #CaskAle. Add situational ones for events, beer styles, and seasons. See the hashtag bank in Tab 3 for the full list.

9

Event Promotion System This Month

Every recurring event — Cask Club Wednesday, Pie & Pint Thursday, Whisky Club, Running Club — should have a Facebook Event, an Instagram Story, and a TikTok. Create once, reuse the template weekly, just swap the details. Events are how Facebook pushes your content to non-followers. Use the ready-made templates from Tab 3.

10

Newsletter: "The Blues Brief" Next Quarter

Set up a free Mailchimp account and create a monthly newsletter called "The Blues Brief". Include: next month's events, beer highlights, and a personal note from Cal & Alice. Collect emails with a sign-up sheet in the pub and a link in your bio. Your competitor runs "The Tap Times" — you need yours. Newsletters build a direct line to your most loyal customers that no algorithm can take away.
