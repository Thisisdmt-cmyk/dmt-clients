Your Action Plan

Eight recommendations, prioritised by impact and urgency.

Urgent — do this week
High — this month
Medium — next 3 months
Low — when ready
Urgent #1 Professional Logo
Commission a proper logo that incorporates the name "Blue's" with character. Consider: a beer tap or handle silhouette in blue, or a simple wordmark with a pint glass dot on the apostrophe. It must work at all sizes, on dark and light backgrounds, and in both print and digital. The animated GIF cannot serve as your logo — it fails on signage, merchandise, menus, and business cards.
Urgent #2 Colour Palette
Upgrade from a single blue to a full brand palette:

#1B4B7A Deep Pub Blue — primary, richer and more confident
#D4A853 Ale Gold — warmth, craft, premium feel
#F5F1E8 Warm Cream — for light backgrounds
#0F1923 Midnight — for dark contexts
#2E7D6B Hop Green — highlights, cider connection
Urgent #3 CAMRA Badge Integration
The Pub of the Year 2026 badge should be visible on every single touchpoint: Facebook cover photo, Instagram highlight, website hero section, window sticker, menu boards, Google Business photos, and every social post header. This is the most powerful marketing asset you currently have. Use it relentlessly.
High #4 Professional Photography
Book a half-day shoot covering: venue exterior and interior, beer taps and selection, Cal and Alice behind the bar, events in action, the beer garden, and food (pies, cheeseboard). Professional photos transform every platform you're on — website, social media, Google Business, TripAdvisor, press coverage.
High #5 Brand Fonts
Adopt two brand fonts, both free from Google Fonts:

Playfair Display for headings — classic, with a pub heritage feel
Inter for body text — clean, modern readability

Use these consistently across your website, social media graphics, menus, and any printed materials.
Medium #6 Brand Story
Every great pub has a story. Yours is a good one: opened in February 2020, built by Cal and Alice Watson as a labour of love. A tiny pub with a big heart, founded on real ale, real cider, and real conversation. Five years later — CAMRA's Pub of the Year. The story writes itself. Ready-to-use versions are in the Assets tab.
Medium #7 Merchandise
The CAMRA win makes merchandise viable and desirable. Start with: branded pint glasses, t-shirts ("I drank at Britain's best pub"), tote bags, coasters, and beer mats. Merchandise turns your customers into walking ambassadors and creates a secondary revenue stream. Get the logo right first (Recommendation #1), then the merch follows naturally.
Low #8 Signage Refresh
Once the new logo and colour palette are in place, update your external signage to match. The pub front is the first impression for every walk-in — it should reflect the quality of what's inside. Coordinate with the new brand identity for a cohesive, professional look from the street.

Long (Press, Features, Print)

Click to copy When Cal and Alice Watson opened Blue's Micro Pub in February 2020, the idea was simple: create a space where people could enjoy properly kept real ale and real cider in good company. No televisions, no fruit machines, no pretence — just five handpulls, six ciders, and conversation. Tucked into the heart of Whitburn village in Tyne and Wear, Blue's quickly became more than a pub. It became a gathering place. Regulars mixed with curious visitors. Pies and cheeseboards accompanied pints. Events brought the community through the door, and the warmth of the welcome kept them coming back.

Five years later, that dedication to doing things properly has been recognised at the highest level. In 2026, Blue's Micro Pub was named CAMRA Pub of the Year — the most prestigious award in British pub culture, celebrating the very best of real ale and community-focused hospitality. For Cal and Alice, the award belongs to everyone who has walked through the door and made Blue's what it is. A tiny pub with a big heart, and now, officially, one of the finest in Britain.

Brand Voice Guidelines

How Blue's should sound — everywhere.

Do

  • Be friendly and warm
  • Be personal — use first names
  • Be proud of what you do
  • Sound like a real person, not a brand
  • Celebrate your community
  • Be local — mention Whitburn
  • Be enthusiastic about beer and cider
  • Keep it short and punchy

Don't

  • Sound corporate or formal
  • Use salesy language or hard sells
  • Be pretentious about beer
  • Sound like a chain pub
  • Overuse hashtags or emojis
  • Use generic hospitality phrases
  • Apologise for being small
  • Copy what bigger venues do
