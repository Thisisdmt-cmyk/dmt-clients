What's Already Working

These are genuine strengths to build on — not starting from scratch.

Your Brand Has Real Equity

  • The name "Blue's" is distinctive and memorable. It's personal, it's a colour, and it immediately suggests character. Most pubs would pay good money for a name this strong.
  • 2,200 Instagram followers — more than the national CAMRA winner. Tamworth Tap, a three-time National Pub of the Year with its own brewery, has 1,792. Your two-person operation outperforms them by 23%. That's remarkable.
  • 1,745 Instagram posts show sustained commitment. This isn't a social media account that was set up and abandoned. You're consistently present and your audience is engaged.
  • 4.9 stars on Google (119 reviews) is near-perfect. Your reputation is excellent. People already love what you do.
  • Your tone of voice is authentic and right. Friendly, unpretentious, warm. You sound like a great pub should sound — and that's harder to get right than most people think. Protect this.
  • Double CAMRA branch awards in one year. Pub of the Year and Cider Pub of the Year 2026. That's a marketing asset that most pubs never earn.

Action Plan

Six recommendations to polish what's already working. Not a rebrand — a brand tidy-up.

Urgent — do this week
High — this month
Medium — next 3 months
Urgent #1 Get a Print-Ready Logo
Keep the current logo for social media — it has personality. But commission a clean, scalable version of your mark that works on a pint glass, a business card, a menu, a window sticker, and at 16px as a favicon. It doesn't need to be a totally new design. It just needs to be a proper file (SVG and PNG) that scales to any size. Think of it as the "official" version of what you already have.
Urgent #2 Put CAMRA Front and Centre
Two branch awards in one year is a serious achievement. Make it visible everywhere: Facebook cover photo, Instagram bio and highlights, website hero section, Google Business photos, window sticker, menu boards, and every printed material. Tamworth Tap displays their CAMRA badges prominently across all touchpoints — you should do the same. This is the most powerful marketing asset you currently have. Use it.
High #3 Adopt Your Brand Colours and Fonts
Move from one undefined blue to a complete palette (see the Ready-Made Assets tab — it's already built for you). Pick up Playfair Display for headings and Inter for body text — both free from Google Fonts. Apply these consistently to your Square site, social graphics, menus, and any printed materials. This single change makes everything look intentional and cohesive.
High #4 Unify Your Platform Profiles
Use the same profile photo, the same bio structure, and the same colour scheme across Facebook, Instagram, Google Business, and TripAdvisor. Platform-specific bios are in the Assets tab, already formatted for each platform's character limits. Same visual, same voice, same story — everywhere someone finds you. This is the fastest way to look polished.
Medium #5 Professional Photography Session
Book a half-day shoot covering: the pub exterior and interior, beer taps and selection, Cal and Alice behind the bar, food offerings, and the upstairs games room. Professional photos will transform your Google Business listing, website, and social profiles. One good shoot gives you months of content. Your Instagram output is already excellent — professional photos will elevate it further.
Medium #6 Write Your Brand Story
The story already exists — it just needs to be told consistently. Opened February 2020 by Cal and Alice. A tiny pub built on real ale, real cider, and real conversation. Six years later: CAMRA's best in the branch. Three ready-to-use versions (short, medium, and long) are in the Assets tab. Copy, paste, use.

4. Brand Voice Guide

How Blue's should sound — with examples specific to a micropub.

Do

  • Be warm and welcoming
    • "Come and try the new Thornbridge pale — it's a belter."
  • Use first names — it's personal
    • "Cal's pick of the week is on tap 3."
  • Sound like a real person, not a brand
    • "Quiet one tonight. Perfect for a proper chat over a pint."
  • Be proud of your awards
    • "Still buzzing from the CAMRA news. Thank you all."
  • Celebrate your regulars and community
    • "Cheeseboard Sunday was rammed. You lot are brilliant."
  • Be specific about what's on tap
    • "Three new ciders this week, including a cracking perry from Herefordshire."
  • Keep it short and punchy
  • Mention Whitburn — own your village

Don't

  • Sound corporate or formal
    • "We are delighted to announce our latest beverage offerings."
  • Use salesy language or hard sells
    • "LIMITED TIME ONLY! Don't miss out on our incredible deals!"
  • Be pretentious about beer
    • "Notes of stone fruit with a lingering biscuit malt backbone."
  • Sound like a chain pub
    • "Join us for our Two-for-One Tuesday promotion!"
  • Overuse hashtags or emojis
    • "Best night EVER!! #micropub #realale #camra #beer #whitburn #pub"
  • Use generic hospitality phrases
    • "We pride ourselves on delivering an exceptional customer experience."
  • Apologise for being small
  • Copy what bigger venues do
